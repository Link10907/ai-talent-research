# Whoop — Private Health Data + Partial Social Feed

## Source date range: 2020–2025

## Scale
- Users in 56 countries; subscriber count not publicly disclosed
- Valuation: $1.2B (2020) → $3.6B–$3.7B (2025)
- Revenue: $260M ARR (Latka 2025); some projections suggest $500M+ possible but unconfirmed
- Growth: significant; hardware + subscription model hybrid

## Subscription / Pricing
- Three annual membership tiers (2025): $199 / $239 / $359 per year
- Device included in membership (no upfront hardware cost)
- Model: wearable-as-a-service; subscription = ongoing access to data + device

## D30 / D90 Retention
- Unknown; not disclosed
- Model suggests high retention: canceling = losing device + years of health history
- Annual plans create natural 12-month retention floor
- Estimated: annual renewal rate >70% (industry inference from subscription hardware)

## Privacy Architecture
- Data stored on Whoop cloud servers
- Privacy policy allows sharing "aggregated and de-identified" data for "research and commercial purposes"
- 2025 class action lawsuit (Lomeli v. Whoop, Northern District of California, Aug 2025): alleged Segment tracker embedded in app collected sensitive data without consent
- Washington My Health My Data Act (eff. March 2024): requires opt-in consent for consumer health data

## Social Layer
- Whoop has a community feed: users can share strain scores, recovery scores, workouts
- Default: most data private; social = opt-in
- "Teams" feature: employers/coaches can view employee/athlete data (explicit consent required)

## % Users Public
- Majority private; social sharing is opt-in minority
- No official stat; estimated <20% actively share publicly

## Data Fit with "Feed B-Side Data" Goal
- MODERATE fit: Whoop's aggregated data has research/commercial value
- Risk: 2025 lawsuit shows user sensitivity to covert data practices
- Lesson: explicit opt-in consent + clear B2B use disclosure is essential
