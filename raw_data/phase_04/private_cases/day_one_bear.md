# Day One & Bear — Pure Journal / Note Apps

## Source date range: 2017–2026

## Day One

### Scale
- 15M+ downloads (cumulative)
- ~40K monthly downloads as of March 2026 (SensorTower estimate)
- Revenue: ~$400K/month (est.) = ~$4.8M ARR
- Acquired by Bloom Built, now owned by Automattic (WordPress parent) — acquired 2021

### Pricing
- Basic: free (limited journals)
- Silver: $49.99/year
- Gold: $74.99/year
- Transitioned to subscription model in June 2017

### D30 / D90 Retention
- Unknown; journaling apps typically have low D30 (high churn after initial enthusiasm)
- Journaling category benchmark: estimated 10–20% D30 (lower than productivity tools)
- Power users = high LTV; casual users churn fast

### % Users Public
- 0%: journals are private by design; no public sharing feature
- Some users export and blog separately; no in-app public layer

### Data Realness
- Encrypted sync (optional iCloud or Day One Sync)
- Automattic ownership: data practices aligned with WordPress norms

### Fit with "Feed B-Side Data" Goal
- ZERO fit: personal diary data cannot ethically feed B2B

---

## Bear

### Scale
- Bear is an iOS/macOS markdown notes app
- No official user count published; estimated 1–2M users based on App Store reviews and community size
- Revenue: subscription model (Bear Pro) at ~$2.99/month or $29.99/year
- Small team (~5-10 people); bootstrapped

### Privacy
- Local-first option; iCloud sync E2E encrypted
- No telemetry; no ads

### Fit with "Feed B-Side Data" Goal
- ZERO fit; small niche product

---

## Lesson for Our Product
- Journal/diary apps have natural high churn and low DAU (people don't journal daily)
- Lock-in comes from historical data accumulation, not daily utility
- Neither monetizes data externally; both rely purely on subscription
