# Notion — Private Default, SaaS Collaboration

## Source date range: 2022–2025

## Growth Curve
- 2019: ~1M users
- 2022: 30M+ users (estimated)
- Sep 2024: 100M+ total users (super.so blog citing Notion)
- Paying customers: ~4M (implied ~4% conversion on 100M total)
- Revenue: $67M (2022) → $250M (2023) → $400M (2024) → $500M+ ARR (Sep 2025) → $600M ARR (late 2025 per Latka)
- Valuation: $10B (2021 raise, maintained)

## D30 / D90 Retention
- Not publicly disclosed
- Industry benchmark for consumer SaaS (Lenny Rachitsky): good = 40% at 6-month; great = 70% at 6-month
- Notion likely above average due to deep workspace lock-in; actual numbers unknown

## Monetization Path
- Free tier → Plus ($10/mo) → Business ($15/mo/member) → Enterprise (custom)
- AI add-on layer (Notion AI) driving ARPU uplift since 2023
- >50% of paying customers using AI features by 2025
- ARPU not publicly disclosed; rough estimate: $400M revenue / 4M paying ≈ $100/user/year

## Data Realness
- User data = workspace content (docs, databases, notes)
- Private by default; users can publish pages publicly (Notion Sites)
- No ads; no selling user data to third parties

## % Users Public
- Small minority; Notion Sites (published pages) represent escape valve
- No official stat on % who make content public
- Estimated <5–10% of users ever publish a public page

## Fit with "Feed B-Side Data" Goal
- LOW fit: data stays private, no opt-in B2B data channel
- Notion AI processes user content but does not sell insights to enterprises
- Model lesson: deep lock-in and high switching cost compensate for no B2B data loop
