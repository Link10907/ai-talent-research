# Roam Research — Rise and Decline

## Source date range: 2020–2024

## Growth Curve
- 2019–2020: viral growth in "tools for thought" community; bidirectional linking hype
- 2020–2021: peak adoption; no official user number ever published
- 2022: visible decline begins; Every.to article "The Fall of Roam" (Feb 2022) documents early adopter exodus
- 2023: last Twitter post on product (Sep 2023); marketing effectively ceased
- 2024: small but devoted "Believers" cohort remains
- 2026: ~1M monthly active visitors (per blog.thefix.it.com) — but "visitors" not "active paying users"

## Pricing (unchanged since 2020)
- $15/month standard plan (no free tier)
- Believer plan: $500 for 5 years ($8.33/mo)
- No enterprise plan; no team features worth noting

## Decline Causes
1. No mobile app (iOS/Android) — critical gap vs Notion/Obsidian
2. Slow product velocity; founder Conor White-Sullivan known for long development cycles
3. Community management issues: "negative users purged from subreddit" controversy
4. Obsidian launched free + local-first → captured privacy-conscious power users
5. Logseq (also free, open-source) captured graph-note users
6. Notion added backlinks → captured mainstream users

## D30 / D90 Retention
- Unknown; no data published
- Proxy: Believer plan ($500/5yr) suggests a loyal core; but mass market churn was high

## Data Realness
- Cloud-hosted by default; all notes stored on Roam servers
- No E2E encryption; company can access content
- No known data sales or B2B data products

## % Users Public
- Essentially 0%: Roam is fully private, no public sharing feature
- Exception: users can share individual pages via link

## Fit with "Feed B-Side Data" Goal
- ZERO fit: no B2B data path, declining user base, no public layer
