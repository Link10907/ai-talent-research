# Otter.ai — Meeting Transcription, Private Default

## Source date range: 2023–2025

## Scale
- Users: 14M (2023 estimate) → 25M+ (2025)
- 50M meeting summaries milestone (company blog, 2024)
- Founded 2016; bootstrapped initially, then VC-backed

## Revenue
- $22.3M ARR (2023) → $81M ARR (end of 2024) → $100M ARR (March 2025)
- Growth: 348% revenue increase in two years (2023–2025)
- Milestone: $100M ARR press release, Dec 2025

## Pricing
- Free: 300 min/month transcription
- Pro: $8.33/month (billed annually)
- Business: $20/month/user
- Enterprise: custom; focus on Zoom, Teams, Google Meet integration

## D30 / D90 Retention
- Unknown; not disclosed
- High stickiness expected: integrated into meeting workflows, data accumulates over time
- Meeting-dependent tool: retention tied to meeting frequency; remote work tailwinds

## Privacy Architecture
- Meetings transcribed and stored on Otter servers
- Privacy policy: recordings/transcripts stored, processed by AI
- No known B2B resale of individual transcript content
- Enterprise: admins can access team meeting data (org-level access)

## % Users Public
- Essentially 0%: transcripts private by default
- Shared link option exists (like Google Docs share); not a public feed

## Fit with "Feed B-Side Data" Goal
- LOW-MODERATE fit: aggregate meeting patterns (topics, terminology frequency) theoretically valuable for B2B intelligence
- In practice, Otter has not pursued this path; sensitivity of meeting content makes it politically toxic
- Lesson: meeting content is the most sensitive possible data; B2B data play impossible without massive trust damage
