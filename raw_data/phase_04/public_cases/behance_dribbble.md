# Behance & Dribbble — Designer Portfolio Platforms

## Source date range: 2024–2025

## Behance

### Scale
- 50M+ members (official claim, Adobe)
- ~17M monthly unique visitors
- Owned by Adobe (acquired 2012)
- All portfolios public by default; platform IS the public showcase

### Monetization
- Free for users; Adobe monetizes via Creative Cloud upsell
- Job postings, Behance Pro (limited paid features)
- Not a standalone revenue business; Adobe acquisition synergy

### % Users Public
- ~100%: Behance is a public portfolio platform; private = not publishing

### D30 / D90 Retention
- Unknown; likely low DAU (passive portfolio, not daily usage)
- Portfolio "set and forget" pattern: high initial activity, then dormant

---

## Dribbble

### Scale
- 12M users (2025 estimate per designer Dann Petty on X, March 2025)
- Earlier estimates: 4–6M; growth to 12M possible through expanded access
- Historically invite-only → open; transition changed community character
- Privately held; no revenue disclosed

### Monetization
- Dribbble Pro: ~$8/month
- Job board: primary revenue (companies post design roles)
- B2B design talent sourcing: core value proposition

### % Users Public
- ~100%: portfolio platform; purpose = public display
- Private shots possible but rare; defeats the purpose

### Design Pattern We Could Copy
- Portfolio = passive public signal of skill + taste
- Hiring companies pay to access designer pools
- Works for design; equivalent for AI talent = public work samples / project history

### Fit with "Feed B-Side Data" Goal
- HIGH structural fit: public work data → B2B talent intelligence
- Weakness: engagement is passive (portfolios vs active usage)
