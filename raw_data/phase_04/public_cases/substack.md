# Substack — Default Public Newsletter + Notes

## Source date range: 2021–2026

## Scale
- 5M paid subscriptions (March 2025, official announcement)
- 50M total active subscriptions (paid + free) as of 2026
- ~100K publications earning money (April 2026, up from 50K May 2025)
- Growth: 1M paid (2021) → 2M → 3M (2024) → 5M (March 2025)

## % Users Public
- Default = public: all newsletters/posts public by default
- Paid-only posts: behind paywall (visible to paid subs only); still "published" content
- Notes (Substack's Twitter-like feature): public by default
- Writers who restrict all content to paid-only: minority

## D30 / D90 Retention
- Newsletter open rates: Substack average ~40–50% (vs email industry avg ~20%)
- Subscription retention: paid subscribers to top newsletters show high retention (annual renewal >70% for successful publications)
- No platform-level D30/D90 data published

## Monetization Path
- Platform takes 10% of paid subscription revenue
- Writers set prices; readers pay directly
- Substack Pro: advances paid to top writers (minority program)
- No advertising; no data sales

## Design Pattern
- Creator-as-product: writer's credibility = user retention mechanism
- Public posts = SEO + discovery; private/paid posts = monetization
- Substack Notes = public engagement layer that feeds newsletter growth

## Fit with "Feed B-Side Data" Goal
- LOW fit: content is public but not structured data; no B2B data layer possible
- Lesson: public content + direct monetization works but creates no B2B intelligence value
