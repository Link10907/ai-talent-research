# LinkedIn — Default Public Professional Network

## Source date range: 2011–2025

## Scale
- 1.3B+ total members (2024–2025)
- 310M monthly active users (MAU)
- 134.5M daily active users (DAU) — ~43.4% DAU/MAU ratio
- 257M US users; global in 200+ countries

## Revenue
- 2011 (IPO year): Talent Solutions = ~50% of total revenue; Q4 2011 Hiring Solutions = $84.9M (50% of rev)
- 2012: Talent Solutions = 54–55% of total revenue; Q3 2012 = $138.4M
- 2024: Total LinkedIn revenue ≈ $17.1B; Talent Solutions ≈ 60%+ = ~$10.3B
- 2025: Revenue growth 8–10% YoY; Premium segment crossed $2B in 12 months (Jan 2025, TechCrunch)
- Source: SEC Form 8-K filings (FY2011, FY2012); Microsoft earnings (FY2024)

## Monetization Path
- Talent Solutions (Recruiter, Job Slots, Career Pages): 60%+ of revenue → the B2B data layer
- Premium subscriptions (Premium Career, Sales Navigator, etc.): ~15–20% of revenue
- Marketing Solutions (ads): ~20–25% of revenue
- Core insight: user data (profiles, connections, job changes) = raw material for Recruiter product

## D30 / D90 Retention
- Unknown (Microsoft does not report these metrics)
- DAU/MAU = 43.4% suggests strong stickiness for a professional network
- Network effects: leaving = losing your professional graph (high switching cost)

## % Users Public
- Default = public profile (name, headline, photo visible to all)
- Salary data: private (hidden behind aggregated "Salary Insights")
- ~40% of users estimated to have fully public profiles; remainder semi-public or restricted
- No official public/private breakdown stat

## Design Pattern We Could Copy
- Profile data (skills, experience, connections) → Recruiter product that charges employers
- User generates value for B2B buyer without explicit compensation
- Salary transparency data (user-submitted) → aggregated for B2B intelligence

## Fit with "Feed B-Side Data" Goal
- HIGH fit model: this IS the B-side model
- Risk: user awareness of data monetization growing; 2024 backlash when LinkedIn used posts for AI training
