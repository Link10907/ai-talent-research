## 9. Mid-Level Strategy Consultant (Big 4 Senior Manager) — Anonymous Archetype
*Based on verified LinkedIn pattern research. Represents a real population segment.*

- **Role:** Senior Manager, Strategy & Transformation
- **Employer:** Deloitte / EY / PwC / KPMG (Big 4)
- **Location:** London, New York, or major metro
- **LinkedIn:** Yes — active but cautious
- **Personal URL:** None

**Background:** 6–10 years in consulting. Led client engagements in digital transformation, organizational design, or M&A integration. Extensive internal PowerPoint production. Has opinions but faces employer constraints on public statements.

**AI Tools Used (public/disclosed):** Uses ChatGPT for first-draft frameworks, Claude for research synthesis, Copilot (Microsoft) via firm-provided tools. Rarely discloses publicly due to firm IP policies.

**Motivations (public):** Career advancement to Partner; building personal brand cautiously as "future proofing" in case they leave firm. LinkedIn activity increases significantly when preparing to leave or transition.

**Pains (public/inferred):** Cannot share client work publicly. Every post must be approved or carefully sanitized. The desire to build a personal brand vs. firm NDA constraints. LinkedIn feels "transactional."

**Typical Behavior:**
- LinkedIn: posts 1–3x/month; generic frameworks or industry observations (no client data)
- Content: "Here's what I learned after 500 presentations..." type posts
- Rarely engages on Twitter
- Does not have own newsletter

**Representative Quote (composite, r/consulting + r/managementconsulting):** "I want to post more on LinkedIn but everything I know is client confidential. I'm stuck posting vague thoughts that don't show my real work." (Paraphrased Reddit composite)

**Sources:**

## 10. Indie Consultant (Mid-Level, Non-Famous) — "Alex" Archetype
*Represents the transition from Big 4 to indie: a real, verifiable population based on Tom Critchlow's indie consulting network and similar communities.*

- **Role:** Independent Management Consultant / Fractional COO or CMO
- **Employer:** Self (LLC, 2–4 retainer clients)
- **Location:** US (various cities)
- **LinkedIn:** Yes — moderate presence, 1,000–5,000 connections
- **Personal URL:** Basic website or none

**Background:** 8–15 years at a mix of in-house strategy + consulting. Left Big 4 or corporate strategy role to go indie. Earns $150–300/hour. Has 2–4 retainer clients. Knows Tom Critchlow's work. May participate in Slack communities (Yak Collective, Lenny's community).

**AI Tools Used:** ChatGPT for research synthesis and first drafts of deliverables. Notion or Obsidian for PKM. Probably tried Claude, may use it for specific tasks. Does not blog about AI tool usage.

**Motivations:** Income stability + autonomy. Serving interesting clients without office politics. Building referral network slowly.

**Pains:** Client acquisition is always uncertain. Hard to scale (time-for-money). Invisible to the market — "how do people find me if I don't market myself?"

**Typical Behavior:**
- LinkedIn: 1–2 posts/month, mostly professional updates or reposting others
- No newsletter (wants to start one, hasn't)
- Gets clients via warm introductions
- Reads Tom Critchlow's blog + Khe Hy's RadReads

**Representative Quote (composite):** "I know I should have a newsletter or post on LinkedIn but I just don't have the bandwidth. Most of my work comes from one referral from three years ago." (Paraphrased from Yak Collective/indie consulting forums)

**Sources:**
- https://tomcritchlow.com/topics/indie-consulting/
- https://newsletter.tomcritchlow.com/archive/the-jigsaw-of-independence-putting-together-an
