## Signal: Lenny's Newsletter as PM Population Proxy

| Stat | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Lenny's Newsletter subscribers | ~1.1–1.2M (April–May 2025) | lennysnewsletter.com; newsletterinsights.io |
| Lenny's paid subscribers | ~18,000 (earlier data) | Growth in Reverse |
| US PM population estimate | ~200,000–500,000 (no clean BLS bucket) | Zippia / LinkedIn |
| Lenny's reach vs US PM population | **~200–500%** of named PM population; much of subscriber base is founders, marketers, and PMs internationally | |

**Implication:** Lenny's 1.1M subscribers likely reaches far beyond the US PM population — audience is global (PM + founder + marketer + designer). This makes it a poor proxy for "total PM population" but a great example of how creator audiences overshoot any single occupation category.

## Signal: Reddit vs. Total Professional Population

| Subreddit | Subscribers | US Professional Population | Ratio |
|---|---|---|---|
| r/marketing | ~1.3M | ~2.5M (core US marketers) | ~52% of core; ~22% of broad 6M definition |
| r/consulting | ~341K | ~1.1M (US management analysts) | ~31% |
| r/userexperience | ~380K | ~670K (US designers, core) | ~57% |

**Warning on interpretation:** Subreddit subscribers ≠ US professionals only. Most subreddits are global + include students, adjacent roles, hobbyists. The actual US professional penetration is much lower — possibly 5–15%. The ratios above are worst-case upper bounds, not realistic overlaps.
