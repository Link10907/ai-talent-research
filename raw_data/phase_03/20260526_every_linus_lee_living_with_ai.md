# TLDR
Every.to profile of Linus Lee — one of the most detailed accounts of how an AI researcher/designer actually uses AI tools in their daily workflow. Key insight: most tools are custom-built, not off-the-shelf.

## Source
URL: https://every.to/chain-of-thought/linus-lee-is-living-with-ai
Date: 2022–2023 (original profile; still frequently cited)
Author: Every.to

## Key Excerpts

**Custom tools built by Linus:**
- Notation: outliner app that automatically highlights semantically related concepts across notes
- Monocle: full-text search engine across personal data (journals, notes, contacts, tweets, bookmarks)
- Revery: semantic search accessible via Chrome extension for finding conceptually related notes and websites
- CoStructure: visual heatmaps showing most important sentences in articles

**Public tools used:**
- Elicit: academic paper search via question rather than keyword
- ExplainPaper: AI explanations of dense paper sections

**Core AI philosophy:**
"Prompting models are still a kind of dark art."

**On interface design:**
Advocates for "direct manipulation in meaning space" rather than typed prompts — intuitive controls like dragging to adjust text dimensions (formality, length, sentiment).

**Role and background:**
Independent researcher focused on better interfaces for human-AI interaction. Previously Notion, Betaworks, Replit. Now Thrive Capital. Built 100+ personal projects.

**AI as "thought calculator":**
"AI is meant to be a 'thought calculator' — a way to enhance human imagination and creativity, not replace it."
