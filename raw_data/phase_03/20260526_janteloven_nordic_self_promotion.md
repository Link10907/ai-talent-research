## Key Excerpts

**Janteloven core principle:**
"Janteloven encapsulates unwritten rules that dictate social behavior, promoting humility and discouraging individualism, with the essence being to foster community and equality where boasting about personal achievements is frowned upon."

**Ten rules (abridged first five):**
"You're not to think you are anything special. You're not to think you are as good as we are. You're not to think you are smarter than we are. You're not to imagine yourself better than we are. You're not to think you know more than we do."

**US vs Nordic contrast:**
"In high masculinity cultures like the United States, competition and personal achievements are front and center with people openly talking about accomplishments, but in Sweden it's the opposite where humility and collaboration matter more than self-promotion."

"Scandinavians are culturally conditioned not to self-promote — almost to a fault, and rank among the lowest in the world for self-promotion." (Workhuman)

**Career impact in Norway:**
"Individuals hesitating to assert their accomplishments or seek promotions due to fear of being perceived as boastful or arrogant."

**Modern shift:**
"Younger Scandinavians are pushing back more and more against Janteloven — Swedish bloggers note that 'Nowadays, Swedes rather laugh at Jante and his rather depressing rules.'"
"Social media has amplified voices that might have once been silenced by Janteloven's constraints."

**LinkedIn implications:**
"Norwegian audiences appreciate direct, trust-focused messaging that avoids overly promotional language."
"Important to avoid coming across as overly self-promotional — it may not resonate well with Nordic audiences."

**HBR (2014) on professional self-promotion across cultures:**
Dated but: professionals from "low self-promotion" cultures face real career disadvantages when working in US contexts. Recommendations include finding a sponsor, framing achievements as team outcomes.
