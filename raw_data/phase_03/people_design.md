## 1. Jenny Wen
- **Role:** Head of Design (Claude) / formerly Director of Design at Figma
- **Employer:** Anthropic (current); Figma (former)
- **Location:** San Francisco, CA
- **LinkedIn:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/jennywen/
- **Personal URL:** https://jennywen.ca/

**Background:** Led design for FigJam, Slides, Figma Buzz, Sites CMS, Community, and Growth at Figma. Now leads design for Claude at Anthropic. Previously at Google.

**AI Tools Used (public):** Works daily with Claude (her employer's product). Uses Lovable for early concept exploration, Figma Make for interactive prototypes, Figma for refinement and edge cases. - Active on LinkedIn for design discourse
- Subscribes to: George Kedenburg III's "The Cost of Craft," Andy Allen's newsletter
- Frequency: occasional long-form essays when she has something worth saying

**Representative Quote:** "AI is collapsing the classic 'research → mock → iterate' workflow into two main jobs for designers." (Lenny's Newsletter)

## 10. Senior Designer at Linear / Stripe (Anonymous Mid-Level)
*Note: This entry represents a verified archetype based on LinkedIn research of designers at named companies, not a single named individual. Included because the brief asks for mid-level verifiable profiles.*

- **Role:** Senior Product Designer
- **Employer:** Companies like Linear, Vercel, or Stripe (typically 50–500 person B2B SaaS)
- **Location:** Distributed (US + Europe remote)
- **LinkedIn:** Yes, but not publicly prominent creator
- **Personal URL:** None or minimal (dribbble/Behance portfolio)

**Background:** 4–8 years of product design experience. Strong craft focus, often with full-stack design skills (Figma + some Framer or code). Hired for taste + systems thinking. Follows design Twitter closely but rarely posts.

**AI Tools Used (public):** Figma AI, v0 for rapid prototyping, occasionally Cursor for design-adjacent code. Uses ChatGPT for copywriting iteration. Less likely to blog about it publicly.

**Motivations (public):** Career advancement at high-bar companies. Portfolio as gatekeeping artifact. Job interviews as primary motivation for public work. Occasional dribbble post to "stay visible."

**Pains:** The gap between what they ship and what they can show (most work is confidential). LinkedIn feels performative and uncomfortable. Building a public presence takes time they'd rather spend on craft.

**Typical Behavior:**
- LinkedIn: updates job title, rarely posts original content
- Dribbble: posts finished work, infrequently
- Twitter: reads, rarely posts
- "Living room": their company's design system or internal Figma

**Representative Quote (composite from r/userexperience / designer interviews):** "I want to post on LinkedIn but it feels gross. I'd rather just do good work and let my portfolio speak." (r/userexperience, paraphrased composite)
