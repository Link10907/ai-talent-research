# TLDR
Ridd's essay on "Some (slightly nerdy) thoughts on AI and the future of design tooling" — one of the most specific designer-authored pieces on AI workflow philosophy.

## Source
URL: https://www.dive.club/ideas/the-future-of-design-tooling
Date: 2024–2025
Author: Ridd (Richard Ekwonye), Dive Club

## Key Excerpts

**Core thesis:**
"If you want to ship, then you need to assimilate into engineering workflows and generate code your developers are excited to use."

**On designer empowerment:**
"I want to be empowered to contribute to production instead of always having to be reliant on engineers."

**On AI tools in design:**
Tools mentioned: V0, Figma's "First Draft" feature, Dessn. QA (quality assurance) identified as "untapped opportunity" — hidden social cost of QA creates friction at handoffs.

**On AI-era taste:**
"In an AI-enabled era, taste shifts from creation toward curation. Designers would navigate vast design possibilities to select unconventional solutions that still resonate with users — like 'selecting the right bottle of wine for a party.'"

**Workflow quote:**
Treats ChatGPT as a "CTO" (keeps context, breaks project into chunks) and Cursor as an "engineer" (implements).

**Content pipeline:**
Built agentic pipeline with Claude Code: records episode → gets newsletter draft, social posts, show notes, hooks all generated in his voice.
