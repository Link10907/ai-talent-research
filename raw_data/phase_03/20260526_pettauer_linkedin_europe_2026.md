# TLDR
Comprehensive analysis of LinkedIn usage across European countries with cultural engagement patterns. Most useful source for EU regional culture differences in professional display behavior.

## Source
URL: https://pettauer.net/en/linkedin-usage-europe-2025-2026
Date: 2026 (analysis covers 2025–2026 data)
Author: pettauer.net

## Key Excerpts

**Country user counts (March 2025):**
- Germany: 22 million users (surpassed Xing)
- France: 34–36 million accounts (+14% active users)
- Netherlands: ranks 2nd globally for avg connections per user (188)
- Sweden: 3.4M, Norway: 2.96M, Denmark: ~3M (Nordic cluster)
- UK: 47.6M registered (~81.8% of adults 18+), ~16.2% daily active

**90-9-1 Participation Model:**
"Approximately 1% (550,000 European 'superusers') post weekly generating most impressions; 9% engage intermittently through comments; 90% consume passively without public contribution."

**Nordic professionals:**
"Show a 67% preference for technical posts in English versus native languages. Long-form articles see approximately 30% higher shares than the global average."

**Germany/DACH:**
"Bilingual German/English posts achieve 41% broader reach than monolingual German content."

**France:**
"CEO and founder posts receive four times the impressions of average users — reflecting cultural preference for hierarchical thought leadership."
