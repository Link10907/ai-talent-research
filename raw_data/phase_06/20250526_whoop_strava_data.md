# TLDR: Whoop & Strava — Biometric Data Exchange Models

**Date collected**: 2025-05-26
**Source type**: Sacra, Business of Apps, electroiq, Whoop Wikipedia

## Whoop
- 2.5M members paying $200–360/year
- Continuous biometric monitoring accepted (HRV, sleep, strain — 24/7)
- No major privacy backlash despite intimate data collection
- Revenue: $350M+ ARR; 2025 bookings $1B+
- Key: value delivered DAILY (Recovery Score); can't get score without sharing data

## Strava
- 135M users (2024), 180M (2025)
- Default: ALL activities public (opt-out not opt-in)
- 2% of users pay premium (~2.7M paid subscribers estimated)
- Revenue: $163.4M (2024)
- 2018 incident: Global Heatmap revealed military base locations (CIA, NSA) from user GPS data
- Sells aggregated route data to city governments (Strava Metro) — disclosed, opt-out available

## Key Contrast: Why Whoop Works, Why 23andMe Failed
- Whoop: daily feedback loop → data is functional prerequisite → continuous consent → trust maintained
- Strava: social comparison → public-by-default → low user awareness of secondary use → incident but no collapse
- 23andMe: one-time report → long tail secondary use → company failure → trust evaporated

## Combined Insight
Biometric/behavioral data sharing is HIGH when:
- Data is required for the product to work (Whoop)
- Social comparison drives motivation (Strava)  
- Feedback is continuous and personal (both)

Data sharing becomes HIGH RISK when:
- Company faces existential crisis (23andMe bankruptcy)
- Secondary use is revealed suddenly and at scale (Heatmap incident)
- Users feel they cannot exit or delete

## Confidence
High for Whoop/Strava metrics. Medium for behavioral interpretations.
