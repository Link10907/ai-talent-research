# Read.cv — Shutdown & Aftermath

## Timeline
- **Founded**: 2021 (professional portfolio/network platform)
- **Acquisition**: Perplexity AI acquired team, January 2025
- **Shutdown date**: May 16, 2025
- **Also shuttered**: Posts.cv (microblogging) and Sites.cv (portfolio sites)

## User Data
- **Peak user count**: Not publicly disclosed; no official figure ever released
- **Active users at shutdown**: Unknown — never disclosed
- **Character**: Niche, design/tech creative professional focus; "anti-LinkedIn" aesthetic
- **Product Hunt presence**: Modest upvotes relative to hype; never achieved mainstream scale

## Migration Pattern
- Users scattered to multiple destinations: LinkedIn, Polywork (already shutting), personal sites
- No single successor captured the Read.cv community
- Perplexity hired the team but did not continue the product
- Community mourned on X/Threads; LinkedIn posts eulogizing the product went semi-viral

## Why It Failed
- Could not find path to monetization at scale
- Niche appeal (design/indie tech) too narrow for VC-backed growth
- Competitor LinkedIn too entrenched; new entrants (Polywork, Read.cv) fragmented
- "Beautiful but not useful" critique — great UX, insufficient network effect

## Key Signal for Our Product
- Beautiful professional profile platforms fail without a clear job-market loop
- Users don't migrate if the destination has weaker network effects
- When Read.cv closed: no mass migration to a single successor — users dispersed

## Evidence Quality
- Shutdown date: **High** (widely covered)
- User count: **Unknown** (never disclosed publicly)
- Migration pattern: **Low** (anecdotal; no quantified data)
