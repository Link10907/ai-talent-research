# Twitter → Substack Migration (2022–2024)

## Context
When Elon Musk acquired Twitter (Oct 2022), many journalists, creators, and writers accelerated their move to owned-audience platforms — primarily Substack.

## Scale of Migration
- **No official count** of "Twitter → Substack" migrations
- Substack grew from: ~1M paid subscribers (early 2022) → 5M paid (March 2025)
- Net new Substack paid subscribers 2022–2025: ~4M paid (across all publishers)
- Creator count earning money: grew from ~tens of thousands to 100,000 publications (April 2026)

## Notable Migration Drivers
1. **Twitter policy changes** (2022–2023): subscription suppression, algorithmic shifts, journalist account suspensions
2. **Substack Notes** (April 2023): short-form content feature launched as Twitter alternative
3. **Twitter vs. Substack conflict** (April 2023): Twitter briefly banned Substack links — backfired, drove more creator moves
4. **Paid creator accelerator**: Substack funded migrations with advance deals for top creators

## Migration Patterns Observed
- Journalists (tech, politics, finance) most active migrators
- Newsletter writers used Twitter as top-of-funnel → moved paying audience to Substack
- Creators like Justin Welsh, Dan Koe launched Substacks during this period
- US elections (2022 midterms, 2024 presidential) drove political newsletter surges

## Evidence on Stickiness
- Many Twitter-driven newsletter audiences converted at 3–5% paid rate
- Substack became primary income source for many 10K–100K subscriber creators
- Not a full platform replacement — most creators maintained X presence for discovery

## Evidence Quality
- Substack aggregate growth: **High** (Sacra, Substack published data)
- Individual migration numbers: **Low** (anecdotal; no systematic dataset)
- Migration causation: **Medium** (timing correlation with events)
