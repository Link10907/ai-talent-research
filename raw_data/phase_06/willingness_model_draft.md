## A. Willingness to Pay

### Professional SaaS / Newsletters (High-Evidence)
| Product Type | Price | Conversion | Evidence |
|-------------|-------|-----------|---------|
| Technical newsletter (Lenny) | $150/yr | 4–5% of free subs | High |
| AI coding tool (Cursor) | $20/mo | 36% of trial users | High |
| AI productivity (ChatGPT Plus) | $20/mo | ~5% of registered | High |
| Professional upskilling (Reforge) | $1,995/yr | Unknown | Medium |
| CEO peer community (Hampton) | $8,500/yr | Unknown; 1,000 members | High |
| Fitness wearable (Whoop) | $200–360/yr | N/A (device-bundled) | High |
| LinkedIn Premium | $40–100/mo | Estimated ~15–20% of active users | Medium |

### Key Ranges
- Professional newsletter ARPU: $150–$200/year
- Professional upskilling: $1,000–$2,000/year (sweet spot for career-ROI tools)
- Elite founder community: $5,000–$10,000/year
- AI tools (consumer): $20/month = $240/year (high willingness when daily use)

### Critical Insight
Conversion correlates with **immediacy of ROI proof**:
- Cursor (36%): developers see productivity gain in first session
- ChatGPT Plus (5%): general productivity; delayed proof
- Lenny (4–5%): career signal + community; medium immediacy

## B. Willingness to Migrate

### Platform Migration Evidence
| Event | Migration Volume | Retention |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Twitter → Mastodon (Nov 2022) | 1.8M MAU peak | 10% of registered accounts active |
| Twitter → Threads (July 2023) | 41M DAU day 1 | ~25% retained after 18 months |
| Twitter → Bluesky (Nov 2024) | +13M in 6 weeks | −64% engagement 6 months later |
| Polywork shutdown → elsewhere | Unknown | No successor captured users |
| Read.cv shutdown → elsewhere | Unknown | Users dispersed |

### Key Insight
- Migration is event-driven and shallow: users try, then mostly revert
- No "LinkedIn killer" has succeeded because network effect > product quality
- Successful migration requires: clear trigger event + instant value at destination + existing social graph at destination
- Migration triggers: policy change, shutdown, exclusion, price shock

## C. Willingness to Be Discovered ("Open to Work")

### Signal Rates
| Platform | Signal Type | Count | % of Platform |
|----------|------------|-------|--------------|
| LinkedIn public badge | #OpenToWork | 28–40M | 3–4% |
| LinkedIn private | Open to recruiter | 200M+ | ~22% |
| Wellfound | All candidates | 12M | 100% (platform purpose) |
| GitHub | "for hire" tag | Unknown | Est. 2–5% of active devs |
| X/Twitter | Bio text | Unknown | Est. 1–3% of tech accounts |

### Key Ratios (LinkedIn)
- Public signal vs private: **1:5** (only 20% who are open will show it publicly)
- Social risk of "looks desperate": primary barrier to public display
- Using #OpenToWork badge doubles recruiter contact rate

### Insight
Latent demand (private signals: 200M+) vastly exceeds visible supply (public badges: 28–40M). Any platform that reduces the social cost of being "discovered" captures 5× the audience.

### Key Pattern

Paid > Free for habitual engagement (self-selection + commitment)
Cohort schedule > Always-on asynchronous for completion rates

## E. Data Exchange Willingness

### Evidence Summary
| Product | Data Given | Value Received | Willingness | Backlash |
|---------|-----------|----------------|------------|---------|
| Spotify Wrapped | Listening history | Annual personality report | Very high (200M engaged) | Minimal |
| Strava | GPS + biometrics | Social comparison, leaderboards | High (135M users, default public) | Low (military base incident) |
| Whoop | Continuous biometrics | Daily recovery/strain score | High (2.5M paying $200+/yr) | Minimal |
| 23andMe | DNA | Ancestry + health report (one-time) | High initially (80% opt into research) | Catastrophic (bankruptcy) |
| Apple Health | All health data | Aggregated self-view + provider sharing | Medium (private by default) | Minimal |

### Data Exchange Conditions (When Users Say Yes)
1. **Continuous personal feedback** (Whoop, Strava) > one-time report (23andMe)
2. **Social currency** (Spotify Wrapped = identity) > private knowledge (health records)
3. **Opt-in incremental** (Apple) > all-at-once (DNA swab)
4. **Transparent secondary use** > opaque corporate sale (GSK deal)
5. **User control maintained** > irrevocable transfer (bankruptcy data sale)

## F. Preliminary User Willingness Model (Rough — Awaits Opus Analysis)

### For an AI Talent Profile Product (Target: Tech Professionals)

**Willingness to Pay**:
- Free tier expected (norm established by LinkedIn, Wellfound, GitHub)
- Paid conversion likely 3–8% if value is clear career outcome
- Price ceiling for individual: ~$150–300/year ($12–25/month)
- Team/company pricing: $500–2,000/seat/year (Reforge model applicable)

**Willingness to Be Discovered**:
- ~20–25% of tech professionals are actively open to work at any time (LinkedIn private signal proxy)
- Only 3–5% will show it publicly without reducing social cost
- Reducing social risk → 5× more willingness to signal

**Data Contribution Willingness**:
- High for: project portfolios, skills, work output (professional identity = social currency)
- Medium for: salary history, career trajectory, company details
- Low for: job search status made public to current employer network
- Very low for: data that could harm current employment (stealth job search)

**Migration Willingness**:
- Will try a new platform easily (1–2 clicks)
- Will NOT sustain engagement unless peers are also there
- Trigger events needed: recruiter finds them there, viral moment, shutdown of existing platform

**Evidence Confidence Levels**:
- Pay: Medium-High (analogues: newsletters, AI tools, communities)
- Discover: High (LinkedIn data is direct)
- Data: Medium (Spotify/Whoop analogues; professional data is different from personal)
- Migrate: High (pattern consistent across 5 platforms)
