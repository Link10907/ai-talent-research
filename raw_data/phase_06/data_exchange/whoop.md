# Whoop — Subscription Model & Data Exchange

## Business Model
- **Model**: Subscription required (device is included/discounted with subscription)
- **Pricing (2024–2025)**:
  - WHOOP One: $199/year (~$16.60/month)
  - WHOOP Peak: $239/year (~$20/month)
  - WHOOP Life: $359/year (~$30/month)
  - Previous model: $30/month flat
- **Members**: 2.5 million (2025)
- **Revenue**: $260M+ annually; ARR $350M+; 2025 bookings doubled to $1B+
- **Valuation**: $3.6B (2021); growing

## Data Collected
- Heart rate variability (HRV) continuously
- Sleep stages and sleep performance score
- Strain score (daily exertion metric)
- Recovery score (readiness)
- Blood oxygen, respiratory rate, skin temperature
- GPS (optional, during workouts)

## What Users Get for Data
- Personalized daily "Recovery Score" (0–100)
- "Strain Coach" — real-time guidance on exertion
- Sleep performance feedback
- Weekly and monthly performance reports
- Cohort comparisons (anonymous, by sport/age/sex)

## Data Exchange Willingness Signals
- 2.5M subscribers paying $200–360/year for biometric tracking = very high willingness
- Users accept continuous monitoring (24/7 wear) for health optimization feedback
- Whoop sells anonymized aggregate data for research (disclosed in privacy policy)
- No documented major user backlash on data practices (unlike 23andMe)

## Why This Model Works (vs. 23andMe Fallout)
1. Value is delivered continuously (daily scores) vs. one-time (genetic report)
2. Data use is clearly tied to product function — not opaque secondary use
3. Users choose to share more (cohort comparison) vs. being defaulted into it
4. Subscription model = ongoing consent relationship

## Key Insight
Users will pay $200–360/year AND allow continuous biometric monitoring when:
- Personal feedback loop is immediate, personalized, and actionable
- Value requires data (no data = no score)
- Secondary use is disclosed and abstract (research) vs. corporate (GSK drug development)

## Evidence Quality
- Members and revenue: **High** (Sacra, multiple industry sources)
- Pricing: **High** (public)
- Data exchange willingness: **Medium** (inferred from 2.5M subscribers accepting model)
- Secondary data use: **Medium** (disclosed in policy; magnitude unknown)
