# 23andMe — User Data Exchange Willingness & Fallout

## Peak Scale
- **Users at peak**: 15 million+ (genetic profiles in database; some sources say 14M, others 15M)
- **DNA opt-in to research**: ~80% of users opted into research data sharing program
- **GSK deal (2018)**: $300M investment for access to anonymized genetic data of 5M customers; extended in 2022 ($50M more)

## Bankruptcy (March 2025)
- Filed Chapter 11: March 23, 2025
- **Data acquisition**: TTAM Research Institute (nonprofit, founded by 23andMe CEO Anne Wojcicki) bought DNA database for $305 million
- **Data sold**: Genetic profiles of 15M+ users
- **Regulatory pushback**: 28 state attorneys general filed suit to block transfer without explicit consent
- **Consumer access failures**: Users reported inability to access accounts, delete data, or destroy biological samples during bankruptcy process

## User Willingness to Share Data (Pre-Bankruptcy)
- Initial proposition: $99–$199 for ancestry + health reports → users provided DNA
- 80% opted into research sharing = very high data contribution rate
- Users accepted data use by pharma partners (GSK) for drug discovery
- Low immediate concern about secondary use — primarily cared about personal insights

## Post-Bankruptcy Privacy Fallout
- Harvard Law: existing privacy statements allowed data transfer in bankruptcy — users had no practical recourse
- NEJM analysis: Federal law does not adequately protect genetic data given to private companies
- Public Citizen: Congress urged to update Bankruptcy Code for data assets
- California, NY, and other states moving on genetic privacy legislation

## Timeline of Trust Erosion
| Year | Event | Impact |
|------|-------|--------|
| 2018 | GSK deal announced | Minimal backlash — company growing |
| 2023 | Data breach: 7M user records hacked | First major trust event |
| 2024 | Stock collapsed 99.6% from peak | Business viability concern |
| March 2025 | Bankruptcy filing | Mass user concern; delete requests spike |
| 2025 | TTAM acquires database for $305M | Regulatory challenge; data fate uncertain |

## Key Insight for Data Exchange Model
Users will trade intimate data (DNA) for:
1. Personalized insights unavailable elsewhere (ancestry + health)
2. Low upfront cost ($99–$199 one-time)
3. Trusted brand with clear privacy framing

BUT: Trust collapse is catastrophic and irreversible when:
- Secondary data use becomes visible (GSK deal awareness)
- Platform faces existential failure (bankruptcy)
- Users feel they cannot exercise control (deletion failures)

## Evidence Quality
- User count: **High** (widely reported)
- GSK deal terms: **High** (public announcement + SEC filings)
- 80% research opt-in: **High** (reported in bankruptcy coverage)
- Post-bankruptcy user behavior: **High** (documented in NEJM, Lawfare, NPR)
