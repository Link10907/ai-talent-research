# Apple Health — User Data Sharing & Engagement

## Platform Scale
- Apple Health is pre-installed on all iPhones (1B+ active iPhone users globally)
- No paid subscription for core Health app
- Health+ subscription (Apple Fitness+): $9.99/month or $79.99/year

## Data Sharing Design
- **Default**: Health data stays on device (encrypted, private)
- **Opt-in sharing**: Users must explicitly enable sharing with:
  - Healthcare providers (via Health Records / FHIR integration)
  - Third-party apps (explicit permission per data type)
  - Research studies (via ResearchKit)
  - Family members (iOS 15+: explicit share setup)

## Engagement Patterns (Limited Public Data)
- No Apple-published DAU/MAU for Health app specifically
- 2018 study (pre-Apple Health Records): 52% of patients given provider access used it; only ~26% viewed records more than once/year
- Apple Watch users more engaged: Activity rings generate daily habit formation
- ResearchKit studies (e.g., Heart & Movement Study with Cleveland Clinic): Enrolled 400,000+ participants — suggesting willingness to donate health data for medical research

## User Willingness to Share
- Users share health data with apps when:
  1. There's a clear fitness/tracking benefit (Strava, MyFitnessPal integrations)
  2. Healthcare provider requests it (clinical context)
  3. Research framing with institutional credibility (university/hospital studies)
- Users actively resist sharing when:
  1. Purpose is commercial data monetization
  2. Recipient is unknown or untrusted brand

## Privacy Model Contrast (vs. 23andMe / Strava)
- Apple's privacy-as-differentiation strategy: "What happens on your iPhone, stays on your iPhone"
- This builds trust → users MORE willing to add data to Health app
- Third-party access requires per-permission-type grant → reduces blanket sharing

## Key Insight
Apple Health demonstrates that privacy-first design enables MORE data collection — users trust the vault, so they fill it. The data moat is local, not cloud-based. This is a different model from Strava (social sharing) or 23andMe (institutional research).

## Evidence Quality
- Platform reach: **High** (Apple device install base)
- Engagement stats: **Low** (Apple does not publish Health app metrics)
- Willingness patterns: **Medium** (ResearchKit enrollment documented; general patterns from industry studies)
