# LinkedIn #OpenToWork — Data & Statistics

## Key Numbers
- **Public #OpenToWork badge users**: 28–40 million (sources vary by date)
- **Private "open to work" (backend only)**: 200M+ professionals
- **Total "open to work" signal (public + private)**: 220M+ (CNBC Jan 2025; 35% YoY increase)
- **Feature launched**: 2020

## Breakdown: Public vs. Private
| Signal Type | Count | Visibility |
|------------|-------|------------|
| Public #OpenToWork badge | 28–40M | Everyone (including employers, connections) |
| Private "open to work" | ~180–200M | Recruiters with LinkedIn Recruiter tools only |
| **Total** | **220M+** | Varies |

## Impact on Job Search Outcomes
- Using #OpenToWork feature **doubles** chances of receiving recruiter messages
- Public badge increases recruiter contact likelihood by additional 40% vs. private signal
- Recruiters have mixed views: some see badge as signal of desperation; others use it efficiently

## Self-Reported Job Search Behavior
- 220M "open to work" signals = **~25% of LinkedIn's ~900M member base**
- Suggests very high latent job-search intent among LinkedIn users at any given time
- Passive job seekers (private signal) outnumber active seekers (public badge) ~5:1

## Key Insight for Our Product
- Massive population (200M+) is privately signaling job interest but unwilling to broadcast
- Public disclosure carries social risk ("looks desperate") — 5× more people hide it vs. show it
- Reducing social cost of "open to work" = key unlock for talent discovery platforms

## Evidence Quality
- Total "open to work" (public + private): **High** (CNBC citing LinkedIn data, Jan 2025)
- Public badge count: **Medium** (range 28–40M across sources)
- Behavior outcomes (doubling recruiter contact): **Medium** (LinkedIn's own claim)
