# Wellfound (Formerly AngelList Talent) — Job Seeker Openness Data

## Platform Scale
- **Active candidates**: 12 million (2025)
- **Active candidates (earlier 2024 figure)**: 10 million+
- **Profile mix**: ~50% software engineers
- **Active job listings**: 150,000+ from startups

## Openness Rate
- **Platform design**: All candidates on Wellfound are explicitly job-seeking or open to work
- **"Open to work" rate**: Effectively 100% by platform design — joining Wellfound = signaling availability
- Candidates can set specific role types (full-time, part-time, contract, remote only)
- Salary expectations and desired roles are also self-disclosed

## Differentiation from LinkedIn
| Feature | LinkedIn | Wellfound |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| "Open to work" signal | Optional, 28–40M public | 100% of users (platform purpose) |
| Audience | All professionals | Startup-focused job seekers |
| Employer type | All companies | Startups/growth-stage tech |
| Privacy of availability | Public or recruiter-only | Public on platform |

## Revenue Model
- Recruiter subscriptions (employer-side)
- Wellfound is free for candidates
- No paid tier for job seekers

## Key Insight
Wellfound proves willingness to publicly disclose job availability is near-100% when:
1. Platform context is explicitly about job search (reduces social awkwardness)
2. No social risk from existing colleagues seeing the signal (startup-only, separate from LinkedIn)
3. Specific opportunity matching is expected

## Evidence Quality
- User count: **High** (platform marketing / press coverage)
- "Open rate": **High** (structural — platform design)
- Salary/role disclosure: **Medium** (reported feature, no completion rate data)
