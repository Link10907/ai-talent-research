# TLDR: Hampton — Elite Founder Community Economics

**Date collected**: 2025-05-26
**Source type**: community.inc profile, Sam Parr X posts, Fast Company

## Key Numbers
- Members: 1,000+ (July 2024 and Jan 2026)
- Annual fee: $8,500/year
- ARR: $8M
- Core groups: 95+ groups of 5–8 members
- New groups: 10–20 added per quarter
- Member revenue requirement: $3M ARR (raised from $1M, Aug 2024) or $10M past exit
- Average member company revenue: ~$23M/year

## Revenue Math
1,000 members × $8,500 = $8.5M → consistent with reported $8M ARR
Implied churn: very low (< 6% annual)

## Retention Mechanics
1. Small peer groups (5–8 people): deep accountability bonds
2. Exclusive gate: losing membership = losing peer group, not just content access
3. High-quality signal: being a Hampton member = proof of $3M+ business
4. Monthly peer groups: recurring touchpoint, not just Slack access

## Key Insight
At $8,500/year, Hampton is 4–5× more expensive than Reforge. It works because:
- The value is peer relationships, not content
- High gate ensures peer quality
- Small groups create non-replicable bonds

## Confidence
High for revenue/member count (multiple sources cross-referenced). Retention rate: unknown.
