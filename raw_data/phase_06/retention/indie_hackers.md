# Indie Hackers — DAU/MAU & Retention

## Platform Overview
- Founded by Courtland Allen; acquired by Stripe in 2017
- Community for indie founders building profitable products
- No paywall; free community with forum + interviews

## Known Metrics (Limited Public Data)
- **No official DAU/MAU published** by Indie Hackers or Stripe
- Alexa/SimilarWeb estimates (2022–2023): ~500K–1M monthly visitors
- Forum posts: thousands per week; active threads visible publicly
- Stripe has not monetized IH directly — strategic acquisition for developer community goodwill

## Engagement Pattern
- Heavy lurkers vs. active posters — typical community Pareto (90/9/1 rule)
- 1% create; 9% engage; 90% read silently
- Product launch posts and milestone threads drive engagement spikes
- "How I hit $10K MRR" format is signature content driving shares

## Key Retention Drivers
1. Milestones and revenue transparency (public MRR displays)
2. Peer learning without gatekeeping
3. Founder community identity
4. No ads; no algorithm manipulation

## Limitations
- IH has been criticized for declining activity post-2022 (AI tools shifted discussion to X/Twitter)
- Some top creators moved to their own newsletters (Substack)
- No payment → no hard retention mechanism

## Evidence Quality
- Traffic estimates: **Low** (third-party estimates)
- Engagement patterns: **Low** (qualitative observation)
- DAU/MAU: **Unknown** (not disclosed)
