# Maven — Course Completion & Retention Data

## Completion Rates
- **Maven cohort-based completion**: ~75% (May 2021 internal data, cited in analyses)
- **Cohort-based platforms generally**: 85–96% completion vs. 5–15% for self-paced
- **MOOC/self-paced benchmark** (Coursera, edX): 3–15% completion
- **Maven model**: Live cohort sessions + synchronous community → higher completion

## Platform Scale
- **Total students**: 20,000 (as of early 2025; Maven's claim as "largest live learning platform")
- **2023 course completions**: 15,000 (Maven Analytics year-in-review — note: may refer to Maven Analytics, separate company)
- **Course price range**: $500–$2,000 typical; some $200–$5,000+

## Repeat/Return Rate
- No public data on repeat enrollment rate
- Course structure: typically 4–6 week cohorts
- Evidence of repeat: community features (alumni networks) suggest ongoing engagement
- **Evidence quality: Unknown** — not disclosed

## Why Cohort Model Works for Retention
1. Social accountability (peers see your progress)
2. Synchronous deadlines (can't defer indefinitely)
3. Instructor availability (live Q&A, office hours)
4. Community persistence (Slack/Discord post-cohort)

## Key Insight
75–96% completion vs. 3–15% self-paced is a 5–20× improvement. The mechanism is social commitment + schedule structure, not content quality alone. This is the relevant model for any professional community product.

## Evidence Quality
- 75% completion: **Medium** (2021 data, sourced from analysis)
- 20,000 students: **Low** (Maven's own marketing claim)
- Repeat rate: **Unknown**
