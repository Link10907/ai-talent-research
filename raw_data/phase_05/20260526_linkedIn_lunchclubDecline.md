## TLDR
Analysis of Lunchclub's gradual fade (not dramatic shutdown). Core thesis: Lunchclub completed its COVID-era purpose and faded as real networking resumed. Key insight: "AI can match profiles. It struggles to convey reputational signal." Senior professionals require trust built through context, not algorithmic serendipity. Structural limitations: thin contextual information, no mechanism to deepen relationships beyond introduction, no shared professional language or mutual references. Telling metric: "no backlash or collective mourning — users simply quietly migrated elsewhere." No successor platform emerged; users adopted different strategies (domain-specific communities, deliberate warm intros, reduced networking investment). Conclusion: "maturity often looks like subtraction, not replacement."

## Raw Notes
- "Quiet demise" not dramatic shutdown — still technically operational
- Core product limitation: matching is not trust-building
- AI matching ≠ reputational context for senior professionals
- No community formed around the product (transactional utility, not community membership)
- Post-COVID: in-person networking reformed, removing core use case
- Lunchclub's failure mode: low retention, no community formation, temporary demand
- Alternative behavior: users became more intentional about networking, not platform-dependent
- Key quote: "maturity often looks like subtraction, not replacement"
- Implication for any networking platform: you need stickiness beyond the introduction itself
