## TLDR
Mark Gainey interview reveals Strava's true cold-start story. Founders met at Harvard rowing (1980s); "virtual locker room" concept dates to 1995 business plan but lacked technology. July 2008 (pre-launch): recruited 20 cyclist friends to test prototype with Garmin devices during Tour de France. First customer "David" — still with Strava 12–13 years later. Strategy: "We had one customer...We had to figure out every time he uploaded to Strava, how did he have fun?" Early 2009: "just the six of them telling their friends and getting them to sign up, and they had to sign them up because they didn't even have that functionality yet." Target: "MAMILs" (middle-aged men in lycra) — passionate road cyclists willing to pay. Segments feature emerged from user conversations about iconic local climbs. Premium: $6/month. SF Bay Area location helped — VC community full of cyclists who understood the audience and were willing to invest.

## Raw Notes
- July 2008 prototype test: 20 friends, Tour de France timing, Garmin GPS
- "One customer" early principle — obsessive single-user focus before scaling
- "Inch wide, mile deep" targeting strategy
- Segments: organic, from user conversations about local iconic climbs
- Monetization proof: showed investors data from first half of 2010 — users' willingness to engage and pay
- Premium price: $6/month
- Key quote: "building consumer social is like doing a startup on Level 900 Difficulty Mode" — Peter Johnston (Polywork, not Strava — attributed to Johnston elsewhere)
