## TLDR
Most complete secondary source on Substack's founding and early growth. Founded May 2017 by Chris Best (CEO, Kik co-founder), Jairaj Sethi (CTO, Waterloo), Hamish McKenzie (Chief Writing Officer, journalist). All three met at Kik. Core belief: traditional media was broken by Craigslist (classifieds) + Google/Facebook (ad revenue), subscription model could fix it. First customer: Bill Bishop (Sinocism). McKenzie leveraged personal journalism connection; Bishop had 30K subscribers writing about China for free. October 18, 2017: Bishop sent paywall migration email to 30K — "brought in six figures of revenue by the end of that day." Platform validated on day one. YC W2018. Second wave: Daniel Ortberg (The Shatner Chatner), anonymous "Petition" (bankruptcy). Public registration: early 2018. Paid subscribers: 11K (Jul 2018) → 50K (2019) → 100K (Aug 2020) → 500K (Feb 2021) → 1M (Nov 2021). a16z Series A $15.3M (2019) — some capital used to recruit high-profile writers. Business model: 10% commission + 2.9% + $0.30 Stripe fees.

## Raw Notes
- Bill Bishop was recruited via personal journalism connection (McKenzie)
- "Six figures of revenue by end of that day" — validates 10% model on day one
- Early second writers: Ortberg, Petition (anonymous) — not just famous names
- YC W2018: legitimacy + investor network
- a16z invested partly to fund writer recruitment (unusual for a platform, not a content company)
- Writers own their subscriber lists — portability as trust signal
- Growth: 2018→2021 = 90x in paid subscribers in 3.5 years
- Top 10 writers: $7M/yr → $20M/yr (2020→2021)
