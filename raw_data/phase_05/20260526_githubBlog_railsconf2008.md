## TLDR
GitHub's first major public promotion was at RailsConf 2008 in Portland. The company had a booth in Engine Yard's space with t-shirts, stickers, and a banner. All four founders spoke in separate sessions: "The Profitable Programmer" panel (all three GitHub devs + Geoffrey Grosenbach + Ben Curtis), "Dos and Don'ts of Real Life Deploys" (Chris Wanstrath), "Build Your Own Distributed, Self-Configuring Rails Cluster" (Tom Preston-Werner + KirinDave), and "Using Git to Manage and Deploy Rails Apps" (Scott Chacon). No user numbers were mentioned. The post confirms GitHub's community launch was deeply embedded in the Ruby/Rails developer world at a major conference where the Rails framework itself was simultaneously migrating from Subversion to GitHub.
