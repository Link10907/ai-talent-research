# Read.cv — Failure / Acqui-hire Analysis
**Citation IDs: V425–V429**

## Overview
- Founded: 2020 (CEO Andy Chung says 2020; TechCrunch says 2021)
- Founder: Andy Chung — product designer, former Facebook, Mozilla, Quip
- Platform: "show don't tell" professional network — portfolio/resume sharing, professional identity, team profiles, job listings
- Acquired by Perplexity AI: announced January 17, 2025; began winding down operations same day
- Team size at acquisition: ~3 employees
- Backed by: F7 Ventures, Fanjul Capital

## Timeline
- 2020/2021: Launch as minimalist portfolio/CV tool — grew organically among designers and engineers
- 2022–2023: Expanded from CV tool to full social platform — posts, teams, community features; "Sites" feature (personal websites with .cv domains)
- 2023: Reported 100,000+ community members (designers, engineers, artists, builders)
- January 2025: Acquired by Perplexity; platform began winding down

## Peak Metrics
- 100K+ community members (self-reported, year unspecified — likely 2023 or 2024)
- No public revenue/MRR data disclosed
- Team remained tiny (~3 people) suggesting no scaled monetization

## Why Perplexity Acquired It
- Talent acquisition, not product acquisition: CEO Aravind Srinivas — "incredibly capable in designing and building consumer and social experiences"
- Perplexity expanding into enterprise/corporate features and needed design + social product talent
- Third Perplexity acquisition after Carbon (data connectivity) and Spellwise (mobile app development)

## User Reactions (Hacker News — item #42742241)
- Predominantly negative/disappointed
- JadoJodo: "Users are increasingly treated like stepping stools for founders...users help grow a product...get comfortable with that product, integrate it into their lives...and then it's yanked out from under them."
- pixeldrifter: Called the acquisition "a betrayal" — focus shifted to "cashing out your idea" rather than best product for community
- tobr: Called the shutdown schedule "aggressive" (though noted users could download their CVs as a Next.js project for self-hosting)
- dorian-graph: "Yet again, let down by a centralised platform" — suggested decade-long forums more reliable than startup services
- Andy Chung (founder): Called announcement "incredibly bittersweet for us, as the start of this new chapter will mark the end of our time with @read_cv"
- Alternative platforms cited by users: Reactive Resume (open-source), twigg.social, tini.bio

## Multi-Factor Cause of Death

**Product**
- Never found a monetization path; stayed a 3-person team with no revenue model
- Platform identity was unclear: portfolio tool? Social network? Career tool?
- "Sites" feature (personal websites) was interesting but didn't solve the core network-effect problem

**Market**
- Tiny team = no chance to compete with LinkedIn's full-featured professional graph
- Creative professionals (designers/engineers) are a valuable but shallow TAM for professional networking
- No recruiter-side demand = no B2B revenue path

**Team/Capital**
- ~3 employees — impossible to build network effects at this scale
- Small funding (F7 Ventures, Fanjul Capital — undisclosed small amounts); no large rounds
- Talent acquisition by Perplexity was the most rational exit available

**Timing**
- Launched when "aesthetic LinkedIn alternative" was trending; declined as novelty faded
- 2024–2025 AI wave made joining a generative AI company more attractive than continuing small social platform

## Key Lesson for Us
- Aesthetic and design-quality are necessary but not sufficient; professional platforms need a clear utility loop that brings users back with or without their network
- A 3-person team cannot fight the network-effect moat — you need a niche where the existing moat doesn't apply (LinkedIn doesn't serve, e.g., AI talent in a specific function)
- Being acqui-hired at a tiny exit means users paid the cost (disruption/loss) while founders got a soft landing — ethically murky, damages trust in the broader category
