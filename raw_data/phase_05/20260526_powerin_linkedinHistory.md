## TLDR
Comprehensive LinkedIn timeline. Reid Hoffman (former PayPal COO) conceived LinkedIn in December 2002 in his Palo Alto living room with 4 co-founders. Launch: May 5, 2003. Day 1: ~4,500 members — "almost entirely the personal and professional contacts of the five co-founders." December 2003: 81,000 members (entirely organic, invitation mechanics). April 2004: 500,000. August 2004: 1,000,000 (15 months post-launch). Sequoia Series A: November 2003 ($4.7M, pre-money valuation $10–15M). Greylock Series B: October 2004 ($10M). LinkedIn Jobs: March 2005 (first premium product/freemium begins). First profitable month: March 2006. LinkedIn Recruiter: March 2008 (dominant B2B revenue stream for next decade). 2010: 100M members. 2023: 1B+ members.

## Raw Notes
- Hoffman: former PayPal COO, not just "PayPal mafia" — he was an operating executive, not just an investor
- 4,500 day-1 users = friends/contacts only; demonstrates "founder network" cold-start
- Invitation mechanics drove organic growth from 4.5K to 81K in 7 months (18x)
- Sequoia invested before LinkedIn had a business model (Nov 2003 = 6 months post-launch)
- Freemium started March 2005 (2 years post-launch) — free platform for 2 years before premium
- Recruiter as B2B product launched March 2008 = 5 years after launch
- Key data source: Official LinkedIn press releases (confirmed from primary sources)
