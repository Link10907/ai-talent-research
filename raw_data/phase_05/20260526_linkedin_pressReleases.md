## TLDR
Three primary source press releases confirming LinkedIn's funding and product timeline. (1) November 2003: Sequoia Series A — $4.7M, pre-money valuation $10–15M. (2) October 2004: Greylock Series B — $10M. (3) March 2008: LinkedIn Recruiter launch — "premium features and services designed to work intuitively to fit individual corporate staffing departments' needs." These are primary sources that confirm the dates and amounts. The Sequoia investment came just 6 months post-launch, at ~80K users — Sequoia bet on network trajectory, not current revenue. LinkedIn Jobs (first premium product) launched March 2005; first month of profitability: March 2006.

## Raw Notes
- Sequoia A (Nov 2003): confirms Reid Hoffman had investor relationships from PayPal days
- Greylock B (Oct 2004): confirms rapid institutional interest — doubled down on LinkedIn at 1M users
- Recruiter launch (Mar 2008): "Corporate Solutions product" — B2B from day one of enterprise product
- These three releases span LinkedIn's first 5 years: seed→1M users→enterprise product
- Free platform for 2 years before any premium feature (2003–2005)
- B2B pivot with Recruiter in 2008 defined the next 15 years of LinkedIn's revenue model
