## TLDR
Substack hit 1 million paid subscriptions in November 2021 — up from approximately 250,000 in December 2020 (4x growth in one year). The milestone came with a caveat: Substack had "zero profits" at this point despite the scale. Top 10 writers collectively generating $20M in annual revenue (up from $7M year prior). High-profile writer recruitment context: Glenn Greenwald (The Intercept), Matt Taibbi (Rolling Stone), Matt Yglesias (Vox), Andrew Sullivan (New York magazine), Marc Stein (NYT). These migrations signaled editorial independence as a growth vector. Substack's own take at 10% meant the platform earned roughly $2M/yr from top 10 writers alone — but needed to scale to hundreds of thousands of writers to be a real business.

## Raw Notes
- 1M paid subscriptions: November 2021 (across all Substack newsletters, not 1M to one newsletter)
- Zero profits despite 1M subscriptions — reveals the economics of 10% take rate
- December 2020: 250,000 paid → November 2021: 1,000,000 = 4x in 11 months
- Mechanism: COVID-era creator migration + high-profile writer recruitment
- Top 10 writers: $20M revenue; Substack's cut = $2M (before costs)
- High-profile poaching funded partly by a16z Series A capital
- The "1M" is subscriptions not subscribers — one person can pay for multiple newsletters
