## TLDR
Most detailed secondary source on GitHub's early founding story. Tom Preston-Werner and Chris Wanstrath were Ruby programmers frustrated by gatekeeping in open-source contributions. First commit: October 19, 2007. PJ Hyett joined January 2008. Public launch: April 2008. Key freemium insight: Geoffrey Grosenbach (PeepCode) voluntarily offered payment in April 2008 — founders built the paid tier in response to user demand. Chris Wanstrath quote: "In other words, we'd charge the people asking to be charged." By February 2009: 46,000 repos. By July 2009: 100,000 users + 90,000 repos (95% increase in ~5 months, entirely word-of-mouth). GitHub hosted 2M+ repos before accepting any VC (2012). No paid marketing in first 4 years.

## Raw Notes
- Founding frustration: Rails contributions controlled by gatekeepers who prioritized connections over merit
- First commit: Oct 19, 2007
- Freemium was reactive, not planned — user asked to pay, founders said yes
- "Charge the people asking to be charged" — Chris Wanstrath (key quote)
- 100K users by mid-2009 = ~14 months post-public-launch
- Zero VC until 2012 ($100M Series A, a16z)
- Business model confirmed: free public repos, paid private repos — from launch
