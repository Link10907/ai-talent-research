## TLDR
Path (social network): launched November 14, 2010 by Dave Morin (Facebook design lead), Dustin Mierau, Shawn Fanning (Napster). Raised $66.2M total (not $77M as sometimes cited). Key events: 1.5M downloads in first 3 weeks; Google offered $100M at 3 months (Morin declined); reached 2M users after Nov 2011 redesign; February 2012 privacy scandal (uploading address books without permission); FTC $800K fine Feb 2013 (underage data); lost most Western users by 2014; sold to Daum Kakao May 2015 (price undisclosed); shut down September 2018. The failure was multi-factor: "intimate" design limited viral growth; privacy scandal contradicted core value prop; failed to attract beyond tech crowd; path to monetization unclear; without fresh VC the company ran down.

## Raw Notes
- Rejection of $100M Google offer at 3 months = key inflection point
- "Failed to attract users outside the tech crowd" — classic niche trap
- Privacy contradiction: intimate/trusted network uploading contacts without permission
- Retreated to Asia (Korea, Indonesia) — sign of failure in home market
- $66.2M burned on a product that never monetized at scale
- Acquisition price undisclosed; the brief's "$80M" figure is unconfirmed
- Dave Morin departure after Daum Kakao acquisition
- Shutdown: Sep 2018 = 8 years after launch
