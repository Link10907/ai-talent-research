# GitHub — Cold Start Data (2008–2012)
**Citation IDs: V400–V404**

## Founders
- Tom Preston-Werner, Chris Wanstrath, PJ Hyett, Scott Chacon
- Preston-Werner and Wanstrath were Ruby programmers who felt frustrated contributing to Rails (gatekeepers prioritized connections over merit)
- First commit: October 19, 2007. PJ Hyett joined Jan 2008. Public launch: April 10, 2008 (after closed beta)

## First ~100 Users — Where From
- First users were personal and professional contacts of founders, all from the Ruby on Rails developer community
- RailsConf 2008 (Portland) was a pivotal early promotion: GitHub had a booth in Engine Yard's space, t-shirts, stickers, and all four founders spoke in separate talks ("The Profitable Programmer" panel featured all three GitHub developers)
- Rails framework itself migrated from Subversion to Git/GitHub in mid-2008, making GitHub the home of Rails — its top repository — which drove mass community adoption
- First paying user: Geoffrey Grosenbach (PeepCode) volunteered payment in April 2008; founders built freemium in response to user demand, not premeditated strategy

## First ~1,000 Users — Where From
- Word-of-mouth within Ruby/Rails community entirely; no paid marketing
- GitHub at RailsConf 2008: four talks, prominent booth presence
- By February 2009 (first year anniversary): 46,000 public repositories, 17,000 formed in the previous month
- By July 2009: surpassed 100,000 users with 90,000 repositories — 95% increase achieved entirely through word-of-mouth

## Key Product Decisions (First 2 Years)
- Freemium model from day one: unlimited free public repos, paid subscription for private repos ("charge the people asking to be charged" — Chris Wanstrath)
- Public-by-default: social coding, forking as first-class feature
- No venture capital until 2012 ($100M Series A from Andreessen Horowitz) — bootstrapped entirely on subscription revenue
- Gist (code snippet service) launched at a Ruby conference 2008

## Time to 100K and 1M Users/Repos
- 100K users: by mid-2009 (roughly 14 months post-launch)
- 1M repositories: July 25, 2010 (27 months post-launch)
- 1M users: approximately 2010 (within the same timeframe)
- 2M repositories by 2011; 3M users + 5M repos by Jan 2013

## Initial Monetization Model
- Freemium: free public repos, paid private repos
- No venture capital through first 4 years — profitable on subscription revenue alone
- First paying customer in month one (April 2008)

## Invite-Only?
- No — open registration from public launch (April 2008)
- Closed beta for a few weeks before public launch, but no sustained invite-only period
