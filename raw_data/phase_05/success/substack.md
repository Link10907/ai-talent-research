# Substack — Cold Start Data (2017–2021)
**Citation IDs: V415–V419**

## Founders
- Chris Best (CEO, co-founded Kik messenger), Jairaj Sethi (CTO, Waterloo CS), Hamish McKenzie (Chief Writing Officer, former journalist)
- All three met at Kik. Founded May 2017.
- McKenzie brought journalism connections; Best brought product/distribution expertise from Kik

## First ~100 Users (Writers) — Where From
- First customer: Bill Bishop, author of Sinocism (30K subscribers, newsletter about China)
- McKenzie leveraged personal journalism connection to recruit Bishop
- October 18, 2017: Bishop sent email to 30K subscribers announcing paywall migration — brought in six figures of revenue by end of that day
- Platform validated its 10% take-rate model on day one
- Early second-wave: Daniel Ortberg's "The Shatner Chatner," anonymous bankruptcy industry newsletter "Petition"
- Y Combinator W2018 batch — gave access to Silicon Valley network and legitimacy

## First ~1,000 Users (Writers) — Where From
- Active outreach to journalists and newsletter writers (Hamish McKenzie cold outreach strategy)
- Andreessen Horowitz Series A ($15.3M, 2019) — some capital specifically used to bring high-profile writers onto platform
- High-profile poaching: Glenn Greenwald (The Intercept), Matt Taibbi (Rolling Stone), Matt Yglesias (Vox), Andrew Sullivan (New York magazine), Marc Stein (NYT)
- Strategy: land credible writers → demonstrate their success → attract more writers

## Key Product Decisions (First 2 Years)
- 10% take rate (plus 2.9% + $0.30 Stripe processing) — simple, transparent, creator-aligned
- No advertising model — pure subscription; protected writer independence
- Writers own their subscriber lists (portable, not locked into Substack)
- Public registration opened early 2018 (first few months was invite-only for writers)
- YC W2018: gave platform legitimacy and early investor network

## Growth Milestones (Paid Subscribers Across Platform)
- July 2018: 11,000 paid subscribers
- 2019: 50,000 paid subscribers
- August 2020: 100,000 paid subscribers (COVID accelerated creator-to-newsletter migration)
- February 2021: 500,000 paid subscribers
- November 2021: 1,000,000 paid subscribers (announced publicly)
- Top 10 writers: $20M collective annual revenue by 2021 (up from $7M year prior)

## Funding Timeline
- 2018: Seed — The Chernin Group, Twitch CEO Emmett Shear, Zynga co-founder Justin Waldron, Zhen Fund
- 2019: Series A — Andreessen Horowitz ($15.3M)
- Later rounds: valuation grew significantly on 1M paid subscriber milestone

## Initial Monetization Model
- 10% of subscription revenue (writers set their own price; Substack takes 10%)
- Standard payment processing fees on top
- Zero monetization on free tier — pure alignment with writers' paid success

## Invite-Only?
- No formal invite-only for readers
- Writers had to apply / be invited initially (2017–early 2018)
- Open public registration for writers from early 2018 onward
- Strategic "big name recruitment" continued in parallel with open access
