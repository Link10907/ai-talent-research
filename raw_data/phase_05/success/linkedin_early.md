# LinkedIn Early History — Cold Start Data (2003–2008)
**Citation IDs: V410–V414**

## Founders
- Reid Hoffman (former COO of PayPal), Allen Blue, Konstantin Guericke, Eric Ly, Jean-Luc Vaillant
- Conceived December 2002 in Hoffman's living room in Palo Alto, CA
- Hoffman was part of "PayPal Mafia" — connected to Peter Thiel, Elon Musk, Max Levchin

## First ~100 Users — Where From
- Launch day (May 5, 2003): ~4,500 members — "almost entirely the personal and professional contacts of the five co-founders"
- Primarily PayPal alumni, Silicon Valley professionals, and tech-adjacent networks
- Peter Thiel (PayPal co-founder) was an early investor

## First ~1,000 Users — Where From
- Organic growth through invitation mechanics (early platform was partially invite-based)
- By December 2003: 81,000 members — "almost entirely organic through invitation mechanics"
- By April 2004: 500,000 members
- By August 2004: 1,000,000 members (15 months post-launch)

## Key Product Decisions (First 2 Years)
- Real professional identity (not anonymous) — trust and reputation as foundation
- Invitation-based growth mechanics built early social proof
- LinkedIn Jobs formally launched 2004 — first commerce surface
- Freemium model introduced March 2005 (LinkedIn Jobs as first premium service)
- Recruiter product launched March 2008 — became dominant revenue stream for next decade
- First month of profitability: March 2006

## Time to Milestones
- 4,500 members: Day 1 (May 5, 2003)
- 81,000 members: December 2003 (7 months)
- 500,000 members: April 2004 (11 months)
- 1,000,000 members: August 2004 (15 months)
- 100,000,000 members: 2010 (7 years)
- 500,000,000 members: 2017
- 1,000,000,000 members: 2023

## Funding Timeline
- Series A: November 2003 — Sequoia Capital led, $4.7M, pre-money valuation $10–15M
- Series B: October 2004 — Greylock led, $10M
- Series A fundraising story: LinkedIn was growing fast enough that Sequoia competed for the deal

## Initial Monetization Model
- Freemium model (professional features gated)
- LinkedIn Jobs (March 2005): first premium product
- LinkedIn Recruiter (March 2008): enterprise hiring tool — "premium features and services designed to work intuitively to fit individual corporate staffing departments' needs"
- Established as dominant B2B revenue stream post-2008

## Invite-Only?
- Yes, early days were effectively invite-only (friends-of-founders network)
- Evolved to freemium open registration with premium tiers after reaching scale
