# Eulogy for Read.cv (Facundo Matiaude on Medium)
URL: https://medium.com/@facumatiaude/eulogy-for-a-product-that-really-cared-read-cv-and-posts-cv-say-goodbye-1b793741715f
Date: February 16, 2025
Author: Facundo Matiaude (designer)

## Key verbatim quotes
1. "Read.cv is a show, don't tell professional network with beautiful profiles and meaningful connections."

2. "It never felt overwhelming. Just the right amount of interaction, like a conversation instead of a broadcast."

3. "They built a culture of trust — where quality mattered more than quantity, by setting a precedent for warmth and care."

4. "They proved that it is possible to build around heart and community, and hopes aren't lost for crafting authentic, organic spaces."

## Context
- Author describes himself as in the "denial stage of grief"
- Notes community-wide reaction: "writing saddened posts," "memes of digital grief"
- Reports users discussing migrating community to Bluesky

## Significance
This is the strongest user-side qualitative evidence about what designers/creatives actually want from a professional network:
- "Show, don't tell" (work-evidence-based, not credential-based)
- "Beautiful profiles" (design control, aesthetic)
- "Meaningful connections" (curation > scale)
- "Conversation, not broadcast" (anti-engagement-bait)
- "Quality > quantity" (anti-volume)
- "Warmth and care" (anti-performative)

Each of these is a precise inversion of a LinkedIn pain point. This 5-bullet list is essentially the spec for what 2-3% of LinkedIn-dissatisfied users want — and the same list of features Polywork/Read.cv/Bento all tried and couldn't sustain at scale.
