# Polywork Postmortem (Prompt-Led Product Substack)
URL: https://promptledproduct.substack.com/p/what-happened-to-polywork-an-eye

## Five reasons identified by author
1. **Network effect underestimation** — couldn't achieve critical mass
2. **Weak differentiation** — "Unique features aren't enough; your product needs a clear, compelling value proposition"
3. **Confusing pivot** — Switched from LinkedIn competitor to website builder, alienated early adopters
4. **Funding pressure** — $13M raised pushed unsustainable growth
5. **Market timing** — Launch during "tech fatigue"

## Quoted user feedback
- Antonio Avalos: "people don't want many websites or portfolios to visit just a standardized view in a trusty place."
- Benedikt Kantus: "They never provided sufficient additional value over LinkedIn."

## Key insight on LinkedIn moat
LinkedIn's dominance stems from its established network containing "current and former bosses, colleagues, alumni, potential future employers." Breaking this requires not just better features but solving the "cold start problem"—users won't join platforms lacking existing connections.

## Founder quote (Peter Johnston, on X, Dec 2024)
"after 3.5 years, I made the call to shut @Polywork down and return the remaining capital to investors."
