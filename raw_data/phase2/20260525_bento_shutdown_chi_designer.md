# Bento.me Shutdown — Designer Reaction
URLs:
- https://chisenires.design/blog/goodbye-bento/ (designer Chi Señires)
- https://alternativeto.net/news/2025/12/bento-to-shut-down-in-2026-as-linktree-takes-over-and-offers-migration-path/
- https://x.com/AlternativeTo/status/2002784384542249458 (announcement)

Date: Shutdown announced Dec 2025, effective Feb 13 2026

## Facts
- Bento.me (link-in-bio tool with grid-based content, acquired by Linktree ~2 years ago) sunsetting Feb 13 2026
- After shutdown: all user data + content permanently deleted, Bento links redirect to Linktree
- Linktree previously did the same thing in early 2024 with another acquired product

## Chi Señires (designer) — verbatim
"Just got the email that Bento is sunsetting soon and that it'll shut down on February 13, 2026, so now I've just made an update to remove the link in my Contact page and wherever else I set it up. It was a nice 3 years of having it up, since the visual widgets were cool, but now I guess I don't have a need for another linktree."

"I still have omg.lol which works more or less the same 😆"

## Bento team
"We've had the privilege of seeing so many of you create incredibly beautiful and creative Bentos and form a supportive and positive community."

## Significance
This is the third LinkedIn-adjacent identity platform to die within 14 months (Polywork Dec 2024, Read.cv May 2025, Bento.me Feb 2026). Each was loved by a niche of designers/creators. Each could not reach scale.

The user reaction here (Chi) is notable for what it ISN'T: outrage. It's resigned. "It was a nice 3 years." The designer's lived experience is that link-in-bio / portfolio products come and go, and the durable identity has to live somewhere they own (omg.lol — a personal website-style service with text content the user owns).

This reinforces: the durable answer for engaged users is NOT another centralized identity platform — it's a personal website. Any new product needs to position itself as something LinkedIn cannot do, NOT as a "better LinkedIn."
