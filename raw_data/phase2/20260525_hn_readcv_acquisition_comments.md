## Themes
1. **Trust crisis**: Users feel exploited when platforms acqui-hire and shut down. This is a major moat for LinkedIn (perceived permanence) AND a wound that new entrants must overcome.
2. **Self-hosting flight**: A non-trivial share of dissatisfied users are converging on self-hosted / open-source solutions, not just other SaaS.
3. **Longevity bias**: "I trust forums and platforms that have lasted 10+ years." A new entrant has cold-start credibility problems on top of the network-effects problem.
4. **Founder incentive misalignment**: Repeated theme that founders cash out, leaving users stranded — explicit ask for better governance.

## Implication
For our product: solve the trust problem early (data portability commitment, export tools, "we won't sell to a search engine" promise) or accept that ~20-30% of the most engaged target users will refuse to invest in another centralized professional identity platform.
