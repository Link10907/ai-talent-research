# "I Gave Up on LinkedIn. Should You?" — Karola Karlson
URL: https://karolakarlson.com/i-gave-up-on-linkedin-should-you/
Date: June 2025
Author: Karola Karlson — marketing consultant, ran ads for 30+ brands, teaches marketing courses

## Primary complaints

**AI-generated content saturation**
- 54% of LinkedIn long-form posts were AI-generated as of October 2024 (per article)
- Platform "drowning in slop"—cheap, copied content rewritten by AI

**False expertise claims**
- Criticizes users without real experience framing themselves as industry experts after 10 minutes daily
- Thousands comment "prompt" seeking cheatsheets without questioning authors' actual achievements

**Questionable "vibe marketing" posts**
- Observed viral posts claiming ability to create "20 GREAT ads in 10 mins WITH JUST VIBES"
- As an experienced marketer, assessed these AI-generated creatives as lacking value proposition or CTA

## Timeline
- May 2024: feed filled with "grindset and broetry" posts
- Jan-Apr 2025: tried active posting, found engagement declining vs. 5-6 months prior
- Other marketers confirmed engagement drops

## Results of her own LinkedIn effort
- Newsletter growth slower than guest articles
- ZERO consulting projects or course sales originated from LinkedIn as first touchpoint over 6 months
- All revenue came through referrals and newsletter subscribers

## Decision
Giving up on LinkedIn for 3 months minimum, keeping account only for professional networking and DMs.
