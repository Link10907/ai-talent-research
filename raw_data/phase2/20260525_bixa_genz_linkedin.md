# BIXA Research: Is LinkedIn Failing Gen Z?
URL: https://bixaresearch.com/blog/2020/11/6/genz-linkedin
Date accessed: 2026-05-25

## Key statistics
- 96% of Gen Z users (ages 19-24) rarely or never use LinkedIn despite over 50% having accounts
- Most accounts were created under pressure from parents or universities, not by choice

## Primary complaints from Gen Z
**Feelings of inadequacy:** Gen Z job-seekers report feeling overwhelmed comparing their minimal experience to established professionals. One user noted seeing "robust profiles of highly experienced individuals" while struggling to frame summer jobs and internships as meaningful professional experience.

**Limited perceived value:** Young users view LinkedIn as "purely transactional"—they enter, search for jobs with "surgical precision," then leave. They see no functional difference between LinkedIn and other job boards like Indeed.

**Content consumption mismatch:** Gen Z doesn't recognize LinkedIn as social media and avoids its news feed entirely, despite spending 3+ hours daily watching videos on other platforms.

## Usage pattern
Gen Z uses LinkedIn exclusively for job searching, while older users leverage networking, content consumption, professional development, and lead generation. The younger cohort demonstrates no engagement with LinkedIn's social or thought-leadership features that drive value for established professionals.

## Caveat
Article dated 2020 — Gen Z then was 19-24, now (2026) early/mid 20s mid 30s. Findings may have shifted but the structural mismatch is consistent with newer reports.
