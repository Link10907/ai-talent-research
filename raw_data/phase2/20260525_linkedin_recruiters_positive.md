# LinkedIn for Recruiters/HR — Positive Voice
Sources: LinkedIn Talent Solutions, SHRM, GoBeyond 2025 recruiter survey, HRD Asia
Date accessed: 2026-05-25

## Headline Statistics
- 87% of recruiters still rely on LinkedIn for sourcing (GoBeyond 2025)
- LinkedIn Recruiter provides unlimited access to 1B+ professionals
- LinkedIn Hiring Assistant: 4 hours saved per role, 62% fewer profiles reviewed, 69% higher InMail acceptance
- SHRM 2025 Talent Trends: 89% of HR pros using AI say it saves time
- LinkedIn Future of Recruiting 2025: gen-AI users recover ~20% of work week (~1 full workday)

## Key Voice (industry analyst)
"It's a miraculous solution for recruiters" — HRD Asia coverage of LinkedIn Hiring Assistant

## Implication for Phase 2
On the demand side, LinkedIn isn't just preferred — it is the data layer. For our B-end (which our product will eventually feed), the LinkedIn moat is real: recruiters' workflow, ATS integrations, InMail templates, search filters, and the candidate graph all converge on LinkedIn.

This is a major design constraint:
- A new C-end product cannot compete with LinkedIn as a recruiter sourcing tool
- But that's not what we're building — we're building a candidate-side identity layer that piggybacks on existing networks (GitHub, personal site, X, Substack)
- The recruiter side of our value chain bypasses LinkedIn via direct B2B sales, not by competing for recruiter mindshare
