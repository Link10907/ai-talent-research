# Entrepreneur.com: One-Quarter of Jobs Posted Online Are Fake Ghost Jobs
URL: https://www.entrepreneur.com/business-news/one-quarter-of-jobs-posted-online-are-fake-ghost-jobs-study/496683
Date: September 3, 2025
Underlying source: ResumeUp.AI analysis of LinkedIn job postings

## Key numbers
- 27.4% of all U.S. LinkedIn job listings are likely ghost jobs
- Top cities by ghost-job %:
  - Los Angeles: 30.5%
  - Philadelphia: 30.1%
  - Indianapolis: 27.8%
  - New York: 26.7%
  - San Francisco: 26.0%
- Lowest: Seattle 16.6%, Boston 18.7%, Dallas 20.1%
- International comparison: Canada 24.9%, UK 14.2%, Australia 10.9%
- Average time-to-fill: 41 days in 2024

## Why companies post them
Congressional Research Service definition: "online job postings for positions that do not exist, or that employers are not planning to fill immediately."

Reasons per HR surveys:
- 43% want to project growth
- 62% want to make current employees feel replaceable
- 38% maintain board-board presence
- 36% test job descriptions
- 26% build talent pools

## Frustration scale
- 93% of HR pros engage in ghost-job posting (45% regularly, 48% occasionally)
- 75% of applicants get no response (Human Capital Institute)
- 81% of job seekers feel stressed about finances during search (Aerotek)

## Anonymous paralegal quote (Interview Guys)
"Over a year later, I'm working as a cashier to make ends meet, still trying to land my first professional role. Despite sending out thousands of applications, the silence I've received is deafening."

## Significance
This is concrete, quantitative damage to LinkedIn's core utility (job discovery). It's the strongest functional complaint, distinct from cultural/aesthetic complaints. A new product can't fix this (it's an employer behavior problem, not LinkedIn's choice), but it weakens trust in LinkedIn as a credible employment platform.
