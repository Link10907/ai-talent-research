# Sherwood News: LinkedIn Has Become an Obsession of Top Corporate Brass
URL: https://sherwood.news/business/linkedin-has-become-an-obsession-of-top-corporate-brass-what-gives/
Date: September 25, 2024
Source type: Business news article

## Core argument
Executives have flooded LinkedIn with inauthentic, copy-paste storytelling content under the banner of "thought leadership." Marketing budgets, remote-work isolation, and AI writing tools have lowered the barrier.

## Verbatim quotes pulled
1. John Hickey, BestOfLinkedIn creator:
   "They are abusing this copy-and-paste strategy of these tall tales of business that are supposed to make them stand out, but all they are actually doing is becoming more of the same."

2. Joe Jaffe, marketing exec at Perception:
   "Marketing with decent reach disguised as thought leadership."

3. Avi Goren, Marqii CEO (on why he posts):
   "Even in B2B, our restaurant partners don't want to buy from a business. They want to buy from another human."

4. Olivia Walch, Arcascope CEO (skeptical):
   "If this is advancing the goals of your company, keep doing it. If you are doing it just to fit in...you're just wasting time."

## Stats cited
- 23% increase in CEO posts since January 2024
- Follower engagement up 31% on those posts
- AI writing tools cited as lowering the friction

## Significance
This piece is one of the more credible "LinkedIn is broken" articles because it interviews actual executives and platform observers (not anonymous Reddit rants). It supports the "performative authenticity" pain point.
