# Beehiiv, Ghost, and Kit (ConvertKit) Platform Data

**TL;DR (150-250 words):** Three serious Substack alternatives serve different creator profiles. Beehiiv (founded 2021 by ex-Morning Brew team) targets serious business-minded newsletter operators who want 0% platform fees, robust analytics, an ad network, and a creator-to-creator acquisition marketplace called Boosts. It hit $30M ARR by June 2025 on a $225M valuation with 90,000 customers. Ghost is the independent/open-source option: no VC pressure, founded 2013, structured as a non-profit (no shareholders), profitable from year one, charges pure SaaS fees (no rev share), and reached $10.4M company revenue in 2024. Kit (formerly ConvertKit, rebranded October 2024) is the largest of the three by revenue ($43.2M ARR), targets multi-channel creators who sell digital products alongside newsletters, and serves 600,000+ creators across many content types. Key strategic distinction: Beehiiv competes directly with Substack on creator-owned audience building; Ghost competes on publication-grade features for independent publishers; Kit competes on the full creator business stack (email + products + automations). None has Substack's discovery/social network, which is the single largest differentiator Substack holds.

## Beehiiv

### Company Background
- **Founded:** 2021 by Tyler Denk and Ben Hargett (both former Morning Brew)
- **Headquarters:** New York City
- **Funding:** $82.7M total; Series B $33M (April 2024)
- **Valuation:** $225M (2025)
- **Investors:** NEA, Lightspeed Venture Partners, Sapphire Ventures

### Financial Metrics

| Date | ARR |
|------|-----|
| June 2023 | $3M |
| May 2024 | $13M |
| December 2024 | $19.8M |
| June 2025 | $30M |

Growth rate: 52% in 6 months (Dec 2024 → June 2025)

Source: sacra.com/research/beehiiv-at-30m-year/ (July 2025 analysis)

### Revenue Breakdown
- **SaaS subscriptions:** ~67% (~$20M)
- **Ad Network + Boosts:** ~33% (~$10M)
  - Ad Network: Brands (Netflix, Notion, HubSpot) pay for newsletter placement at CPM rates
  - Boosts: Creator-to-creator acquisition marketplace; Beehiiv takes 20% commission

### Business Model Details
- Tiered SaaS pricing starting at **$49/month**
- **0% platform fee** on paid subscriptions (unlike Substack's 10%)
- Creator tools: subscriber segmentation, A/B testing, automations, SEO optimization, referral programs
- Scale: **90,000 customers**, **75,000+ newsletters**, **350M monthly readers** (per Beehiiv's own blog; treat with some skepticism as company-owned figure)
- Emails powered: 20+ billion in 2025

Source: sacra.com, beehiiv.com/blog/inside-the-newsletter-evolution-of-2025

### Key Advantages vs. Substack
- No 10% revenue cut on paid subscriptions
- Superior analytics and segmentation
- Multi-revenue stream (ads + subscriptions + boosts)
- API access and white-labeling options

### Key Disadvantages vs. Substack
- No built-in discovery/social network
- Higher learning curve for beginners
- SaaS fee even at early stage ($49/month minimum)

## Ghost

### Company Background
- **Founded:** 2013 by John O'Nolan (ex-WordPress)
- **Structure:** Company limited by guarantee — no shareholders, cannot be sold, no VC backing
- **Funding:** $7.4M total; 100% user-funded after that
- **Profitable:** Since Year 1 of operation

### Financial Metrics (2024)
- **Company revenue:** $10.4M (up from $6.3M in 2023)
- **Publisher subscription revenue generated:** $9.2M in 2024
- **Paying customers (Ghost Pro managed hosting):** 20,000+ active

Source: getlatka.com/companies/ghost, seosandwitch.com ghost-statistics

### Platform Scale
- **Active publishers:** ~12,000 independent publishers using Ghost for newsletters
- **Global installs:** 3M+ (includes self-hosted)
- **Writer adoption growth:** 28% YoY in 2024

### Business Model
- **Zero transaction fees** on subscriptions (unlike Substack's 10%)
- Pure SaaS: Monthly hosting fees based on subscriber count
- Example: 400,000-subscriber newsletter = <$2,500/month; no commission
- 65% of Ghost users offer tiered memberships
- Average ARPU for Ghost publishers: $4.75/month per subscriber
- Top 10% of Ghost creators: $50,000+/year in membership revenue

Source: seosandwitch.com/ghost-statistics, ghost.org/vs/substack/

### Key Advantages
- Open-source; full data ownership
- Zero revenue share ever
- Can be self-hosted (no platform dependency)
- Built-in SEO, membership management, newsletter + web publication in one

### Key Disadvantages
- No discovery network
- More technical setup than Substack
- Smaller creator community/ecosystem

### 2024-2026 Events
- Email open rate on Ghost: 53% (significantly above industry average)
- Ghost CTR: ~11%
- Independent organization structure prevents acquisition — a feature for some creators

## Kit (formerly ConvertKit)

### Company Background
- **Founded:** 2013 by Nathan Barry in Boise, Idaho
- **Rebranded:** October 2024 (ConvertKit → Kit)
- **Structure:** Bootstrapped/private; no known VC funding
- **Status:** Independent, founder-led

### Financial Metrics
- **ARR:** $43.2M (most recent known figure, 14% YoY growth)
- **Total creator revenue facilitated:** $400M+

Source: sacra.com Beehiiv competitive analysis (comparative data)

### Creator Scale
- **Active creators:** 600,000+
- **Emails sent monthly:** 2.5 billion+
- **Emails sent per second (2023):** 902 (70% increase since 2021)
- **Creators earning $100K+:** 18% of surveyed Kit users (2023)

Source: businesswire.com ConvertKit rebrand press release, kit.com

### Business Model
- Tiered pricing: Free plan up to 10,000 subscribers; Creator plan starts at $29/month for 1,000 subscribers
- Sells digital product infrastructure alongside email (courses, templates, etc.)
- No platform fee on subscriptions; charges SaaS monthly fee

### Rebranding Rationale (October 2024)
Per BusinessWire press release: "This rebrand solidifies Kit's commitment to the future of the $250 billion creator economy and the belief that enduring creator businesses are built with email."

Evolution: 2013 as professional blogger tool → 2024 as "email-first operating system for creators"

Source: businesswire.com/news/home/20241001564521 (Kit rebrand announcement)

### Key Advantages
- Most advanced email automation builder among newsletter platforms
- Best-in-class for creators selling digital products
- Large existing creator ecosystem
- Free tier for up to 10,000 subscribers

### Key Disadvantages
- Complex learning curve for automation features
- No built-in discovery network
- No native paid subscription product as polished as Substack's
- Rebranding caused some confusion in the market
