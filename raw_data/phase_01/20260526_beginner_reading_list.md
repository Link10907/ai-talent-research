# Beginner Founder Reading List: 5 Newsletters + 30-Minute Onboarding

**TL;DR (150-250 words):** This file answers the phase brief's Section F: "30 minutes reading list — 5 must-subscribe newsletters to let a product decision-maker quickly onboard to the newsletter ecosystem." The criteria were: (1) the newsletter must help understand the professional newsletter ecosystem's dynamics, not just deliver marketing/design/consulting news; (2) it must be readable in 30 minutes for a complete newcomer; (3) it must represent different creator archetypes and revenue models so the reader develops an intuition for "how this works." The five selected are: Lenny's Newsletter (establishes the gold standard for paid professional newsletter product); Growth in Reverse (reverse-engineers how top newsletters grew — meta-level insight); Why We Buy (mid-tier operator with excellent monetization transparency); Stacked Marketer (shows what a daily digest sponsorship model looks like); and RadReads (consulting/lifestyle segment, shows how a smaller but elite audience generates significant revenue). Together these five demonstrate four different monetization models (paid subs, sponsorship, courses/products, coaching), three different size tiers, and three different content formats. Reading 1-2 issues from each newsletter provides more practical "how this works" insight than reading any generic article about newsletter platforms.

---

## The 5 Must-Subscribe Newsletters (Beginner Founder Onboarding)

### 1. Lenny's Newsletter
**URL:** lennysletters.substack.com
**Subscribe:** Free tier (substantial free content)
**Why first:** Lenny's Newsletter is the clearest example of what a "gold standard" professional newsletter looks like — 1.1M subscribers, $2M+/year revenue, built entirely on word of mouth. Subscribing and reading one issue shows you: (a) the content depth that justifies paid subscriptions, (b) the community layer that makes it stickier than media, (c) how a Substack profile functions as a professional credential.

**Read in 30 min:** Pick any issue from the archive — look at the About page, the subscriber count (visible), the comments section. Notice the Slack community CTA in paid upgrade pitch.

**What you'll understand after:** Why "1 million Substack subscribers" is a meaningful professional signal in 2025.

---

### 2. Growth in Reverse (Chenell Basilio)
**URL:** growthinreverse.substack.com
**Subscribe:** Free tier (substantial)
**Why second:** This newsletter is a newsletter about newsletters — each issue reverse-engineers how a specific top creator grew their newsletter. It's the fastest way to understand the mechanics of newsletter growth (referral programs, cross-promotions, content strategies) without reading 20 separate creator stories.

**Read in 30 min:** Find the Lenny issue or the Katelyn Bourgoin issue — both are free and dense with real data.

**What you'll understand after:** The actual growth mechanics behind the newsletters you're reading, including what's replicable and what's unique to each creator's context.

---

### 3. Why We Buy (Katelyn Bourgoin)
**URL:** learnwhywebuy.com
**Subscribe:** Free
**Why third:** At 63K subscribers and $1M/year revenue from sponsorships + courses, this is the clearest example of a "mid-tier" newsletter operating as a real business. Katelyn is transparent about her numbers (which is unusual) and the newsletter itself demonstrates the "sponsored content" model: how do you integrate sponsors without destroying reader trust?

**Read in 30 min:** Read one issue fully, including the sponsor placement. Notice how the sponsor is woven in vs. clearly separated. This is the template most sponsorship-dependent newsletters use.

**What you'll understand after:** How a 60K-subscriber newsletter generates $1M/year without requiring anyone to pay for the newsletter itself.

---

### 4. Stacked Marketer (Emanuel Cinca)
**URL:** stackedmarketer.com
**Subscribe:** Free
**Why fourth:** Stacked Marketer is the professional digital marketing daily digest. It shows a completely different content archetype from Lenny or Why We Buy — curation + commentary vs. original essays. It's also an example of a newsletter that operates across multiple publications (Stacked Marketer + Psychology of Marketing + Tactics) and reported transparent revenue data via annual reports.

**Read in 30 min:** Read one daily digest. Notice the format: brief items, each with an editorial angle, multi-sponsor format, clean design. This represents the "media company" archetype of newsletter businesses.

**What you'll understand after:** The operational reality of running a sponsorship-funded newsletter portfolio; why open rates (50% for Stacked Marketer) matter more than subscriber counts in ad-supported models.

---

### 5. RadReads (Khe Hy)
**URL:** newsletter.radreads.co
**Subscribe:** Free
**Why fifth:** RadReads represents the consulting/professional-services newsletter archetype: a smaller but elite audience (50K, but includes ex-Goldman, ex-McKinsey readers) that generates outsized revenue per subscriber ($658K peak revenue from 50K subscribers). It also shows the diversified revenue model (newsletter + coaching + courses) and the editorial voice that works for "sophisticated professional" audiences.

**Read in 30 min:** Read one essay. Notice the depth vs. brevity balance — Khe writes longform but each paragraph earns its place. Also note the absence of hard selling; the brand is built on "quality over vanity."

**What you'll understand after:** Why audience quality (not just size) matters; how a 50K-subscriber newsletter can generate more revenue than a 200K-subscriber one if the audience is right.

## 30-Minute Reading Plan

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0-5 min | Subscribe to all 5 (bookmark or use an RSS reader) |
| 5-12 min | Read one Lenny's Newsletter issue + browse the About page |
| 12-18 min | Read one Growth in Reverse issue (Lenny or Katelyn profile) |
| 18-22 min | Scan one Why We Buy issue — focus on sponsor integration |
| 22-26 min | Scan one Stacked Marketer issue — note the curation format |
| 26-30 min | Read one RadReads essay — notice the voice and depth |

**After 30 minutes you should be able to explain:**
1. What makes a "good" newsletter in 2025 vs. a generic email list
2. The three main monetization models (paid subs, sponsorship, products/courses)
3. Why subscriber count alone is a misleading metric
4. How a Substack About page functions as a professional credential
5. What "audience ownership" means and why creators care so much about it

## Why These 5 (Not Others)

**Not included and why:**
- Morning Brew — too media-company-scale to show creator economics; not illustrative
- TLDR — too large and too ad-tech-focused; hard to learn from at that scale
- HBR/McKinsey — institutional; shows nothing about creator economics
- Justin Welsh — best followed on Twitter/X first; newsletter is secondary to his social content

**What's missing from this list:**
- A pure design newsletter (the design newsletter ecosystem is weaker and harder to onboard from)
- A consulting-specific newsletter with strong creator economics (the segment doesn't have one as well-developed as the marketing equivalents)
