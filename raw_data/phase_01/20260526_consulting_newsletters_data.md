# Consulting Domain: Top Newsletter Data

**TL;DR (150-250 words):** The consulting newsletter ecosystem is the most fragmented of the three domains — "consulting" as a category on newsletter platforms doesn't exist cleanly. Instead, consulting-relevant newsletters fall into three sub-types: (1) independent consultant career/lifestyle newsletters (RadReads by Khe Hy, Pathless Path by Paul Millerd, Tom Critchlow's newsletter), (2) strategic thinking newsletters that overlap with management consulting frameworks (Lenny's Newsletter, The Profile by Polina Pompliano, A Smart Bear by Jason Cohen), and (3) legacy institutional content that's distributed via newsletter format (HBR Newsletters, McKinsey Quarterly alerts). The genuinely independent consulting creator space is small: RadReads (50K subs, $658K peak revenue 2022), Paul Millerd/Pathless Path (16K subs), Sari Azout's Check Your Pulse (15K subs), Tom Critchlow (unknown count, influential in indie consulting circles). Subscriber counts are substantially lower than marketing newsletters — the consulting audience is smaller, slower to subscribe to new things, and the topics are harder to make viral. The highest-value consulting newsletters for a product targeting knowledge workers are ones like RadReads (reaches ex-Goldman, ex-McKinsey, ex-Google professionals) and The Profile (Polina Pompliano, "tens of thousands" of highly accomplished subscribers including The Rock and major investors). Key gap: most B2B and management consulting newsletters from firms (Deloitte, BCG, etc.) are informational rather than community-building, and their subscriber counts are not public.

## Independent Consulting & Career Newsletters

### RadReads (Khe Hy)
- **Platform:** Own (newsletter.radreads.co); also on Substack
- **Subscribers:** 50,000+
- **Revenue (2022 peak):** $658,000 gross; $250,000 profit
- **Open rate:** 50-55%
- **Issues published:** 434+
- **Topics:** Productivity, career alternatives, lifestyle design, "high salary earner discovering there's more to life"
- **Frequency:** Weekly
- **Started:** 2015 (left BlackRock to write)
- **Growth event:** CNN feature "Oprah for Millennials" (December 2016) — generated 8,000 subscribers in 3 days
- **Revenue streams:** Coaching ($10,000/engagement), courses (self-paced $1,997; group $7,997), sponsorships

**Audience:** "Investors, consultants, coaches, writers, founders and professors at institutions like Goldman Sachs, McKinsey, the New York Times, Google, and Meta" (self-described by Khe)

**Business model note:** Khe has moved away from cohort-based courses post-2022; revenue has likely declined from $658K peak.

Source: growthinreverse.com/khe-hy/

### Pathless Path (Paul Millerd)
- **Platform:** Substack (newsletter.pathlesspath.com)
- **Subscribers:** 16,000+
- **Topics:** Alternative career paths, independent work, consulting-adjacent career design
- **Frequency:** Weekly (Saturdays)
- **Creator background:** Former BCG consultant, McKinsey (overlap), wrote "The Pathless Path" book
- **Note:** Also associated with StrategyU — an online course for strategy consulting skills. StrategyU is a course product, not a newsletter with independent subscriber count.

Source: newsletter.pathlesspath.com/about, fortelabs.com interview

### Tom Critchlow's Newsletter
- **Platform:** newsletter.tomcritchlow.com (own)
- **Subscribers:** Unknown — not publicly disclosed
- **Topics:** Networked writing, indie consulting, media design, SEO consulting perspectives
- **Frequency:** Irregular (not a fixed weekly cadence)
- **Audience:** Independent consultants, digital strategists, agency founders
- **Profile:** Highly respected within the indie consulting creator community; less mass-market than RadReads
- **Why relevant:** Often cited as the intellectual hub for "independent consultant" identity

Source: subtothis.com/tom-critchlow (aggregator), tomcritchlow.com

### Check Your Pulse (Sari Azout)
- **Platform:** Substack (sariazout.substack.com)
- **Subscribers:** 13,000-15,000
- **Topics:** Tech, startups, future of work — for people who want to "feel human" in the tech world
- **Frequency:** Irregular/occasional (not high-volume)
- **Creator background:** Startup strategist, investor, Colombia-born Miami-based
- **Quote (from first issue):** "While consulting, my ideas were constrained to the people in the room, but writing could reach anyone."
- **Revenue:** Not publicly disclosed; appears to be a byproduct of her consulting practice rather than a standalone business

Source: sariazout.substack.com, checkyourpulse.co/about.html

## Strategic Thinking / High-Level Consulting-Adjacent

### The Profile (Polina Marinova Pompliano)
- **Platform:** Substack (readtheprofile.com)
- **Subscribers:** "Tens of thousands" (exact number not found); Twitter followers: 98,000+
- **Revenue:** Six figures annually (mix of sponsorships and subscriptions at $10/month or $50/year)
- **Topics:** Long-form profiles of the world's most successful people (entrepreneurs, executives, athletes)
- **Frequency:** Weekly
- **Started:** 2020 (quit Fortune magazine)
- **Notable subscribers:** The Rock, Jim O'Shaughnessy
- **Model:** Free core (7-8 profiles/week from outside sources) + paid for exclusive content

Source: newslettercrew.com/blog/three-years-of-profiles, businessofbusiness.com interview

## Why Consulting Newsletters Are Different from Marketing

1. **Smaller total addressable market:** "Consultant" as identity is narrower than "marketer"
2. **Harder to viralize:** Consulting insights are nuanced and long; don't spread via social in the same way
3. **Monetization is indirect:** Most consulting newsletter creators monetize via clients/speaking, not the newsletter itself
4. **Audience is valuable but small:** RadReads reaches McKinsey/Goldman professionals — an audience brands pay premium CPMs for, but there are fewer of them
5. **Community > Content model:** Many consulting professionals prefer communities (Slack groups, cohorts) over broadcast newsletters

**Assessment (high credibility based on cross-referencing multiple sources):** The consulting newsletter space maxes out around 50K subscribers for pure-play independent consulting content. This contrasts with 200K-1M+ for the best marketing newsletters.
