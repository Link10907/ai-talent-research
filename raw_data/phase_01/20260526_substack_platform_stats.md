# Substack Platform Statistics (2024-2026)

**TL;DR (150-250 words):** Substack launched in 2017, raised a $100M Series C in July 2025 at a $1.1B unicorn valuation, and by early 2026 hosts 50 million active subscriptions across 2 million publications. The platform's business model is simple: it takes 10% of all paid subscription revenue and provides writers free hosting, payment processing, and an email list. This has created a flywheel where top creators earn $1M+ annually (50+ of them), which attracts more serious writers. The hard reality underneath the headline numbers: the average paid conversion rate is 3% (Substack's own claim of 5-10% appears inflated), the median creator earns around $4,000/year, and annual churn on paid subscriptions is ~50%. The platform achieved positive cash flow in Q1 2025, suggesting the 10% take rate on ~$450M in writer GMV is working as a business. Key 2025 events: Series C at $1.1B valuation, crossing 5M paid subscriptions milestone, 50+ creators hitting $1M+/year, and the Notes feature driving 32M new subscribers from in-app discovery alone in one quarter. Substack's competitive moat is its discovery network — something Beehiiv and Ghost cannot replicate.

## Platform Fundamentals

- **Founded:** 2017 by Chris Best, Hamish McKenzie, Jairaj Sethi
- **Business model:** Free to publish; 10% platform fee on all paid subscription revenue
- **Primary value prop:** Email list ownership + Substack discovery network + payment processing in one tool
- **Target creator:** Writers, journalists, independent thinkers; 30% have journalism backgrounds

## Conversion & Monetization

**Paid conversion benchmarks:**
- Platform average: **3%** (free-to-paid)
- Niche/specialized publications: **4-10%**
- Tech category leads at **8%**
- Substack's official claim: 5-10% (likely optimistic; real data skews lower)

**Pricing norms:**
- Most common: $5/month or $50/year
- Pro range: $100/year (Lenny's), $150/year (niche professional)

**Creator income distribution (estimated 2025):**
- Median annual earnings: ~$4,000
- Top quartile: $16,000+
- Top 50 creators: $1M+/year
- Nearly 50% of creators: <$500/year

Source: bestwriting.com stats + sacra.com analysis + mazkara.studio earnings analysis

## Retention & Churn

- **Annual churn rate:** ~50% on paid subscriptions
- **Retention at 12 months:** ~35%
- **iOS app drives:** 30%+ of all paid subscriptions

Implication: A creator with 600 paid subscribers loses ~25/month and needs ~357 new free subscribers/month just to stay flat.

Source: sacra.com/research/substack/, multiple creator analysis posts

## Business Model Critique

Substack keeps the 10% take rate in perpetuity — a major friction point for large creators. At $100,000 annual subscriber revenue, Substack takes $10,000. At $1M, it takes $100,000. This drives migration to Beehiiv/Ghost for large creators. Platform risk: "50 publications earn $1M+" but those represent an outsized share of Substack's own revenue.

Counter-argument from Substack: Discovery network creates value that offsets the 10% fee, especially for mid-size creators who rely on recommendations to grow.

Source: platformer.news/substack-series-c-enshittification (Casey Newton analysis), beehiiv comparison page
