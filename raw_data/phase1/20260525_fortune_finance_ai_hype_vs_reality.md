# Fortune — "Is AI really killing finance and banking jobs? Wall Street's layoffs may be more hype than takeover"

Source: https://fortune.com/2025/12/21/is-ai-killing-finance-and-banking-jobs-experts-say-wall-street-layoffs-hype-than-takeover/
Author: Emma Burleigh
Date: 2025-12-21

## Central counter-narrative
AI-driven finance job losses are "largely smoke and mirrors." Banks scapegoat AI to justify workforce decisions actually driven by pandemic-era overhiring and economic uncertainty.

## Key quotes

### Robert Seamans (NYU Stern Center for the Future of Management)
- "AI is often a scapegoat for things, because it's easier to blame AI than softening consumer demand."

### Pim Hilbers (BCG managing director)
- "I think the general headcount trend in banking over the last decade is stable to slightly declining."

### Mike Abbott (Accenture banking lead)
- Banks want AI productivity gains so they "don't have to hire for 24 months."

### Daniel Keum (Columbia Business School)
- "Banking consulting actually not doing too bad" due to low automation tolerance; accounting roles face greater threat.

## Statistics
- 54% of financial jobs have "high potential for automation" (Citigroup).
- 73% of US banking employee time has high AI impact potential.
- 92% of Columbia MBA class 2025 received job offers.
- Harvard MBA offer-less graduates: 4% (2021) → 15% (2024).
- Goldman Sachs headcount: 48,300 (Sept 2025) vs 46,500 (Sept 2024) — slight growth.
- Bank of America added 4 employees; JPMorgan added 2,000 in 2025.

## Significance
Critical counter-evidence to the "AI is hard-replacing junior bankers" narrative. Useful contrarian voice for our report's "反对声音" section — the trend exists but the magnitude is contested.
