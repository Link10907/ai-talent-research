# Recruiter perspective — Resume not dead, but signal-degraded

Sources (compiled):
- https://edzer.org/linkedin-or-resume-what-matters-more-in-2025/
- https://www.index.dev/blog/resumes-are-dying-whats-replacing-them
- https://linkedfam.com/blogs/is-linkedin-becoming-the-new-resume

## Consensus view (recruiter side, 2025)
- Resume is NOT dead. It gets ~6-second scan to decide shortlist.
- LinkedIn open on second monitor for "triangulation" of claims.
- 87% of recruiters use LinkedIn to source candidates (often bypassing the application stack entirely).
- Recruiters increasingly google candidates: "Have they shipped, contributed, written?"

## What's actually replacing resumes for senior/specialized roles
- LinkedIn + external proof points (GitHub, articles, talks, OSS contributions).
- Take-home / work-sample (HackerRank, CodeSignal — coding test invites +86% Jul-Oct 2023).
- For tech: 25% of HN "Who is hiring" postings (May 2026) now require shipped projects / public GitHub / portfolio.

## Pushback
- LinkedIn coverage IS the system; "portfolio sites" don't displace it for most roles.
- Read.cv / Polywork / Contra all failed to scale — strong precedent that LinkedIn's network moat is durable.

## Significance
- Confirms "portfolio over resume" is overstated AS LITERAL REPLACEMENT.
- The real shift is: resume is a filter, but the HIRING decision now needs portfolio-style proof somewhere accessible to a recruiter who's already triangulating across 3-4 sources.
- For our product: the opportunity is NOT to displace LinkedIn but to be a profile that the recruiter triangulates TO (the place "the real work" lives) for non-engineers.
