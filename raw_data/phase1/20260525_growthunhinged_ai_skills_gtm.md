# Kyle Poyar / Growth Unhinged — "Your next job will require AI skills"

Source: https://www.growthunhinged.com/p/your-next-job-will-require-ai-skills

## Headline number
- AI-skill-required postings in tech GTM roles: 65 (Jul 2023) -> ~1,000 (Jul 2025).
- That's a 1,438% increase in two years for non-engineering, customer-facing roles.

## Affected role types (illustrative, non-technical or quasi-technical)
- Social media producers
- Paid search specialists
- BDRs (Business Development Reps)
- Marketing analysts
- Content marketing managers
- Product marketers
- CMOs

## Example JD language
- Bottom: "working proficiency of AI tools like ChatGPT"
- Top: "skills using AI copilots and agents to personalize messaging, automate outreach, and surface insights from CRM data"

## Companies actively hiring AI-native talent
Clay, Vercel, Wiz, Zapier, Brex, ClickUp.

## Specific employer datapoint
- Zapier: "89% of its employees are now 'AI-fluent'."

## What employers actually want
- Curiosity + practical experimentation > specific certifications.
- Quote: "People think you need a big grand vision for how to adopt AI. The reality is that you want as many people playing with this on a personal productivity level as possible."

## Significance
- Strongest single piece of evidence that AI literacy is becoming a hard JD requirement *outside of engineering*. Same direction as Lightcast 51% non-IT figure but with concrete role-level granularity.
