# Indeed Hiring Lab — AI at Work Report 2025: How GenAI is Rewiring the DNA of Jobs

Source: https://www.hiringlab.org/2025/09/23/ai-at-work-report-2025-how-genai-is-rewiring-the-dna-of-jobs/

## Methodology
- Job postings on Indeed (US).
- Skills mapped against GenAI capability via dual evaluation: GPT-4.1 + Claude Sonnet 4.
- ~2,900 skills analyzed.

## Core distribution (job-level)
- 26% of jobs face "high" GenAI transformation potential.
- 54% moderate.
- 20% low.

## Skill-level distribution
- 46% of skills face hybrid (human+AI) transformation.
- 40% minimal transformation.
- 19% assisted transformation.
- 1% fully transformable (≈19 skills classified "very likely" to be fully replaced — up from 0 a year prior).

## Sector cases
- Software dev: 81% of skills are hybrid-transformation.
- Nursing: 68% minimal; but 14% of postings — admin tasks — are hybrid-transformation.

## Key thesis from Indeed
"Work skills themselves won't change, but how they are applied almost certainly will." Credentials may need to evolve rather than be replaced.

## Significance
- Most balanced framing: the change is *in how the work is done*, not in whether the work exists. Argues against a clean "credentials -> portfolios" replacement story.
