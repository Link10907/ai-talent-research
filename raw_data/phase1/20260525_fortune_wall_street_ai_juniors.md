# Fortune — "AI could eliminate entry-level Wall Street banking jobs"

Source: https://fortune.com/2025/06/02/junior-analysts-wall-street-jobs-taken-by-ai/
Author: Sydney Lake
Date: 2025-06-02

## Key claim
Big firms are considering pulling back junior banker hiring by up to two-thirds because AI handles much of the grunt analytical work juniors used to do.

## Quotes

### Christoph Rabenseifner (Deutsche Bank Chief Strategy Officer for Tech/Data/Innovation)
- "The easy idea is you just replace juniors with an AI tool." (notes humans still involved in decisions)

### Jeanne Branthover (Head of Global Financial Services, DHR Global)
- "It's like being in a fraternity and being a pledge. You gotta put in the work to get upstairs." (defending the apprenticeship model)

### Michael Ashley Schulman (Partner/CIO, Running Point Capital Advisors)
- "AI won't eliminate entry-level Wall Street jobs, but it will reduce the number of heads required to accomplish the same task."

### Pushback voices
- Tim Bates (U. Michigan AI prof): AI is "reshaping" roles rather than replacing them; judgment is irreplaceable.
- Michelle Enjoli (career coach): AI will serve as assistant tool short-term, not replacement.

## Significance
Same pattern as legal (Axios article): AI is *thinning* the credential-protected entry pipeline in regulated/elite industries. Implication: the "apprenticeship + portfolio" mid-career identity becomes more valuable when juniors have less institutional experience to lean on.
