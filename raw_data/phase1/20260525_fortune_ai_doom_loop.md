# Fortune — "Trust is at an all-time low": AI doom loop in hiring

Source: https://fortune.com/2025/11/18/hiring-job-seekers-recruiters-talent-acquisition-ai-doom-loop-application-technology/
Author: Nino Paoli
Date: 2025-11-18

## Headline thesis
Both sides — job seekers AND recruiters — are unhappy. AI is destroying signal in both directions.

## Direct quotes

### Daniel Chait, CEO Greenhouse
- "This is the first time I can remember where both sides were unhappy."
- "Trust is at an all-time low for both job seekers and recruiters."
- "You end up basically not being able to tell anyone apart."
- "The solution has to come from better ways to bring out the real interest."

### Paddy Lambros, CEO Dex
- "If you feel like every application you send is kind of a meaningless thing"
- "I can understand the desire for candidates to use AI tools. I just don't think it's very effective."
- "AI usage in first-round interviews is downright insulting and inhumane."

## Statistics

### Trust erosion
- Nearly 50% of US job seekers report decreased hiring trust YoY.
- 62% of Gen Z entry-level workers report lost trust.
- 42% of those who lost trust blame AI directly.
- Only 8% of job seekers believe AI screening is fair.
- 70% of hiring managers trust AI to make faster/better decisions.

### Application volume / signal collapse
- LinkedIn applications spiked 45% YoY; 11,000 applications/minute (June 2025).
- ~50% of job seekers submitting more applications this year (AI-assisted).
- 75% of US job seekers use AI to polish applications.

### Deception
- 41% of US job seekers admit using prompt injections to bypass AI filters.
- 52% of non-users considering it.
- 65% of hiring managers caught applicants using AI deceptively (32% reading from scripts, 22% prompt-injected resumes, 18% deepfakes).
- 36% of job seekers altered appearance/voice/background in video interviews.
- 74% of managers more fearful of fraud than a year ago.

## Significance
This is the single strongest piece of evidence that the *resume-as-signal* is collapsing — not because portfolios won, but because AI saturated the channel. The implication is profound for our product: any new identity surface has to be *trust-bearing* from the start, not just rich.
