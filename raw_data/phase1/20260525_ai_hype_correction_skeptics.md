# AI Hype Correction — Skeptic voices (2025)

## MIT Technology Review — The Great AI Hype Correction of 2025
Source: https://www.technologyreview.com/2025/12/15/1129174/the-great-ai-hype-correction-of-2025/
Author: Will Douglas Heaven, 2025-12-15

### Central thesis
2025 was the year inflated AI expectations were forcibly reset.

### Key data point cited
- MIT researchers found 95% of businesses trying AI implementations found ZERO value (study measured bespoke systems past 6-month pilot stage; excluded informal employee use).

### Quotes
- Andrej Karpathy: chatbots "outperform average humans in many areas but are not better than an expert human" — explains limited workplace disruption.

## GCheck (May 2026) — AI Skills Bubble
Source: https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/05/19/3297597/0/en/63-of-Workers-Admit-to-Exaggerating-AI-Skills-as-Automation-Anxiety-Fuels-an-AI-Skills-Bubble-New-GCheck-Report-Finds.html

### Key finding
- 63% of workers admit to exaggerating AI skills on resumes/profiles — driven by automation anxiety.
- This is HIGHER than Greenhouse's 32% from mid-2025, suggesting the deception trend is accelerating.

## Metaview — How AI is ACTUALLY transforming recruiting
Source: https://www.metaview.ai/resources/blog/how-ai-is-transforming-recruiting-in-2025
- 40% of talent specialists worry automation makes candidate experience cold/impersonal.
- 35% fear AI will overlook candidates with unique skills.
- LinkedIn 2025 research: AI-assisted messaging produces 9% lift in quality hires — "modest" gain vs hype.

## Significance
- The skeptical voice gives needed calibration: AI requirements in JDs are real and growing FAST, but (a) much of this is signaling/marketing by employers, and (b) candidate self-reported AI skills are heavily exaggerated.
- For the report: trend 2 is real in language/JD-counts, but operational impact is contested.
