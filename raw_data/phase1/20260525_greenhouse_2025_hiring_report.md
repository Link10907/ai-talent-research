# Greenhouse 2025 Workforce & Hiring Report (and related coverage)

Source: https://www.greenhouse.com/blog/greenhouse-2025-workforce-hiring-report
Coverage: https://www.unleash.ai/emerging-tech/greenhouse-28-of-candidates-now-use-ai-to-generate-fake-work-samples-and-47-embellish-qualifications/
Coverage: https://hrtechedge.com/greenhouse-report-exposes-a-hiring-system-in-crisis-ai-hacks-fraud-fears-ghost-jobs-and-collapsing-trust/

## Candidate behavior
- 28% admit using AI to generate fake work samples.
- 32% claimed AI skills they don't actually have.
- 45% use AI to prepare for interviews.
- 22% use bots to auto-apply (31% for Gen Z).
- 36% of US job seekers used AI to alter appearance/voice/background in video interviews.
- 26% (45% Gen Z) say AI has made it harder to stand out.

## Hiring manager experience
- 65-74% have caught AI-deceptive applicants (depending on category).
- 91% US hiring managers have encountered or suspected AI-generated interview answers.
- 74% more concerned about fake credentials/deepfakes than a year ago.

## Trust
- 80% of US workers feel insecure in current roles.
- 72% report job differed materially from posting.
- 46% of US job seekers say trust in hiring has decreased over past year; 42% blame AI.

## Quotes
- Daniel Chait (CEO): "Intent will be the new differentiator in hiring." Also: "We don't need more hoops or friction; we need better signals."
- Paaras Parker (CPO): "Transparency, communication, and fairness are competitive advantages."

## Significance
- Confirms that the *static* resume + AI-polished cover letter signal is collapsing — but the implication is NOT that work samples are universally trusted; AI-generated portfolios are themselves part of the fraud surface.
- This is the strongest empirical reason to believe new "trust scaffolding" is needed around how candidates present themselves.
