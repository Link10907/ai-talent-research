# Augment Code — "How we hire AI-native engineers now: our criteria"

Source: https://www.augmentcode.com/blog/how-we-hire-ai-native-engineers-now

## Pitch
Coding ability is no longer the primary differentiator of engineering talent.

## Six dimensions they now evaluate
1. Product & outcome taste (right problems).
2. System & architectural judgment.
3. Agent leverage (structuring work so AI agents execute well).
4. Communication & collaboration.
5. Ownership & leadership.
6. Learning velocity & experimental mindset.

## Interview signals they look for
- Can candidate quickly clarify ambiguous problems?
- Do they recognize architectural risks pre-production?
- Can they direct AND validate AI-generated work?

## Quotes
- "The highest-leverage engineer isn't the one who writes the most code. It's the one who ensures the team is solving the right problem."
- "Think of it as delegation — except your reports are incredibly fast and occasionally confidently wrong."

## Four hiring profiles
- AI-Native Systems Engineer
- AI-Native Product Engineer
- AI-Native Applied AI Engineer
- AI-Native Early Professional

## Significance
- Concrete employer voice — they have *moved past* code-portfolio-as-primary-evidence already; what they want now is a story of judgment + agent leverage. This is hard to convey via a static profile but ideal for a "show how you build" surface.
