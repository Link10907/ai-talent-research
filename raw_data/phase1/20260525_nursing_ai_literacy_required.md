# Healthcare — AI literacy as a baseline nursing competency

Sources:
- https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0029655425001198 (Nursing Outlook, 2025 — N.U.R.S.E.S. framework)
- https://nurseseducator.com/future-nursing-competencies-in-an-ai-driven-world-8-essential-skills-every-nurse-needs-by-2026
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10713739/

## Key findings
- AAACN (American Association of Colleges of Nursing) updated its 2025 Essentials framework to explicitly include informatics + AI competencies for new RNs.
- A September 2025 study found 71% of US hospitals had implemented predictive AI models.
- N.U.R.S.E.S. framework (2025, Nursing Outlook): Navigate AI basics, Utilize AI strategically, Recognize AI pitfalls, Skills support, Ethics in action, Shape the future.

## Required competencies (entry-level RN)
- Data literacy
- Informatics (EHR + AI tooling)
- Critical thinking to assess AI outputs
- Ethics and bias awareness

## Tension
- Individual nurse enthusiasm is high, but a 2025 Frontiers in Medicine meta-analysis found "institutional capacity to deliver structured AI training remains severely underdeveloped."
- So: it's a stated competency requirement but operationally still patchy.

## Significance
- Even in a strongly credential-gated profession (RN license required), AI competency is now layered ON TOP. Confirms the Axios-law pattern: regulated industries don't replace credentials with AI skills — they STACK them.
- Indeed Hiring Lab data point reinforces: nursing has 14% of postings showing hybrid (AI-augmented) transformation, despite 68% minimal-transformation overall.
