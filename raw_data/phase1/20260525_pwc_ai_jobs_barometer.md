# PwC 2025 Global AI Jobs Barometer

Source: https://www.pwc.com/gx/en/services/ai/ai-jobs-barometer.html
Press release: https://www.pwc.com/gx/en/news-room/press-releases/2025/ai-linked-to-a-fourfold-increase-in-productivity-growth.html

## Methodology
- Analysed close to 1 billion job ads + thousands of company financial reports.
- Six continents.

## Hiring-side stats
- 56% wage premium for AI-skilled jobs, up from 25% the year prior.
- Jobs requiring AI skills grew 7.5% YoY even as total job postings fell 11.3%.
- Skills sought in AI-exposed occupations are changing 66% faster (was 25%) — meaning the half-life of skills is collapsing where AI is present.

## Productivity impact
- Productivity growth in AI-exposed industries: 7% (2018-2022) -> 27% (2018-2024).
- 3x higher growth in revenue per employee in most-exposed industries.

## Degree requirement evolution
- AI-augmented jobs: degree-required share fell 66% -> 59% from 2019 to 2024 (-7pp).
- AI-automated jobs: 53% -> 44% (-9pp).
- This is a real, measurable softening of credential requirements — but in absolute terms a slow drift, not a collapse.

## Significance
- Strongest dataset triangulating BOTH trends: AI explicitly required, AND credential requirements thinning, in AI-exposed roles specifically.
- The 56% premium is unusually high — has to be discounted partly as composition effect (AI-required roles skew senior).
