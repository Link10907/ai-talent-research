# Hacker News — "Ask HN: With AI written resumes, what are the new hiring signals?"

Source: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=44730506
Date: 2025

## Themes from comments

### Polish is a red flag
Excessive resume polish itself signals AI generation. Some commenters advise "correct but not too polished sentences."

### Network/referrals dominate
Top voice (user scarface_74):
> "If you are blindly submitting your resume to an ATS, you've already lost."
- Reports direct outreach via recruiters + LinkedIn yielded multiple offers in weeks; hundreds of cold applications yielded nothing.

### Public/OSS contributions help, but only at meaningful scale
- Same commenter notes being the "second highest contributor to a popular official AWS solution" opened doors. Other OSS work went unnoticed.
- Inference: a portfolio isn't enough; *signal density* matters.

### Referrals aren't a silver bullet
- One commenter submitted referrals for qualified SWE friends; saw no priority treatment.

### "Bounty-to-hire" model is emerging
- Some commenters report this as an alt-hiring channel.

## Significance
- Important calibration: HN community already lives in a world where resumes are devalued, but the *replacement* is mostly social/network signal, not portfolio. Portfolio alone, even high-quality, is necessary-not-sufficient.
- HN is unrepresentative of the broader workforce — this is a curated, high-agency, high-network sample.
