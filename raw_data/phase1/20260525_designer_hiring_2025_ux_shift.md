# hubbedin.com — The UX/UI Hiring Shift: What Employers Want in 2025

Source: https://resources.hubbedin.com/blog/the-uxui-hiring-shift-what-employers-want-in-2025

## Core thesis
Resume is necessary but portfolio drives decisions. Portfolios in 2025 must include case studies showing problem→process→impact, not just visual artifacts.

## What the portfolio must contain (vs resume)
Portfolio (where signal lives):
- Case studies with problem-process-impact structure
- Collaborative evidence (annotated artifacts, FigJam boards)
- Metrics showing design results
- Interactive prototypes
- Video walkthroughs
- Personal design philosophy

Resume (supporting, not deciding):
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Business metrics literacy
- Tool proficiency (Figma, FigJam, Miro)
- Soft skills stories

## Quoted prescriptions
- "Employers want designers who can demonstrate WHY a design decision was made, not just how it looks."
- "It's not about listing tools — it's about demonstrating how you use them to collaborate, test, and iterate."

## Significance
- Mainstream non-tech-elite source asserting the portfolio-leads-resume hierarchy for designers.
- This is the closest thing we found to a "portfolio over resume" assertion in 2025 — but note: it's a hubbed/recruiting blog, not a primary research source. Medium credibility.
