# New Products in Professional Identity Space (2024-2026)
**Collected:** 20260526
**Citation IDs:** V123–V127

---

## TL;DR (180 words)
The professional identity product landscape saw several significant events in 2024-2026: Bento.me was killed by Linktree after acquisition; Mirror.xyz was absorbed by Paragraph, consolidating the web3 writing niche; Polywork shut down after its AI website builder pivot failed; and Contra launched Indy AI while surviving as the only meaningful LinkedIn-alternative in the freelancer space. No clear "AI aggregates your professional identity across platforms" startup has emerged as a dominant player — the closest attempts are portfolio generators (AI reads your LinkedIn → generates a personal site) which exist but have not become breakout products. The link-in-bio market consolidated dramatically around Linktree. Among professional website builders, Framer is the clearest growth story — eating Webflow's individual creator market. Substack continued growing as the de facto publishing home for professional writers. The signal: despite billions in AI investment, no one has cracked "AI + professional identity aggregation" at scale. This is either a gap (opportunity) or a signal that users don't want their professional identity aggregated by an AI.

## Platform-by-Platform 2024-2026 Status

### Bento.me — DEAD (Killed by Linktree)
- Acquired by Linktree at unknown date
- All existing accounts permanently deleted (confirmed by search results, 2025)
- Represented: visual grid-based link-in-bio tool with professional identity bent
- Why Linktree killed it: brand consolidation, not maintaining a competitor to their own product
- Lesson: Link-in-bio market has winner-takes-most dynamics. Second-place products get absorbed.

### Linktree — Dominant but Commoditized
- Remains the link-in-bio leader by default
- 50M+ users claimed
- Moving toward commerce features (Linktree Shop)
- Professional identity play: weak. Linktree is perceived as "for influencers" not "for professionals"

### Framer — Growing (Designer-Focused Website Builder)
- Figma-like interface, ships beautiful personal sites quickly
- 2024-2025: Taking Webflow's individual creator market
- Fast performance, clean HTML output
- Best fit: Product designers, UX designers, design-led founders
- Not attempting to be a "professional identity aggregator" — just builds websites

### Webflow — Stable (Enterprise/Team Focus)
- CMS power, AEO/SEO features
- Moving upstream to enterprise
- Less relevant for individual professionals; more relevant for company sites

### Contra — Alive, Repositioned
- Indy AI launched 2025 (AI-powered job/client finding for freelancers)
- 1M+ professionals, 20% growth Q1 2025
- Commission-free freelance marketplace + portfolio profiles
- Not positioned as professional identity tool per se — positioned as freelance business tool

### Wellfound (AngelList Talent) — Zombie/Stable
- Still operating, 150,000+ job listings
- Mixed reviews: "used to be great," "feels like a graveyard" (some users, 2024)
- Niche: startup job seeking
- Not attempting to be a professional identity platform — purely job market

### Paragraph — Growing (absorbed Mirror)
- Acquired Mirror.xyz (May 2024)
- Raised $5M (USV, Coinbase Ventures)
- Web3-native publishing: on-chain subscriptions, NFT-minted writing
- Niche: crypto/web3 writers
- Not mainstream professional identity

### Substack — Strong Continued Growth
- Still the dominant newsletter/writing platform
- Professional identity play: About page as professional homepage for writers
- Substack Notes: attempting to be Twitter/X alternative for writers
- Growing feature set (podcasts, videos, chat)
- Key 2024-2025 data: Lenny's Newsletter (top Substack), Sahil Bloom (The Curiosity Chronicle), Sari Azout (Check Your Pulse) all use Substack as primary publication home

## AI + Professional Identity: Emerging Attempts

### What Exists
1. **LinkedIn AI features (built-in):** AI-suggested profile improvements, AI-written post drafts, AI-matched job recommendations. LinkedIn itself is the biggest "AI professional identity" product but it's incremental feature addition, not a new product.
2. **Portfolio generators:** Tools like "drop your LinkedIn URL → get a beautiful personal website" exist as micro-products (various ProductHunt launches). None have broken out to scale.
3. **Resume AI tools:** Enhancv, Kickresume, Rezi — AI-enhanced resume/CV builders. Not professional identity aggregators.
4. **Profile aggregation pages:** me.sh, about.me, Linktree — simple link aggregators but no AI synthesis of cross-platform identity.
5. **Lessie.ai / Profile.lessie.ai:** Attempted to auto-generate professional profiles from public data. Status unclear.

### What Doesn't Exist (The Gap)
No mainstream product that:
- Pulls in a professional's LinkedIn + GitHub + Substack + personal site + X
- Synthesizes them into a coherent, readable professional identity
- Keeps it updated automatically
- Presents it in a format useful to someone evaluating that person

This gap is either:
- **Opportunity:** No one has built it well yet
- **Signal:** Users don't want AI synthesizing their identity; they want control
- **Technical challenge:** Cross-platform data access is difficult (API restrictions, authentication)
- **Privacy concern:** Users are uncomfortable with their scattered data being aggregated without consent

### Key Observation
Reid Hoffman (LinkedIn founder) conducted an AI deepfake interview of himself in April 2024 — trained on 20 years of public speaking and writing. This represents the extreme end of "AI professional identity synthesis" — and it required 20 years of public data and personal consent/involvement. Scaling this to any professional is a fundamentally different problem.

## Link-in-Bio Market (2024-2025)

Market projected to surpass $1.6 billion by 2026. Key players:
- **Linktree:** Leader (killed Bento after acquiring it)
- **Beacons:** Growing, commerce-first
- **Dotme:** Smaller, professional tilt

These are "front doors" for creators and influencers, not for corporate professionals. The gap: no link-in-bio tool that is *designed for* senior professionals (Director+, executives, consultants). Current tools all feel "creator-coded" — neon colors, influencer templates.
