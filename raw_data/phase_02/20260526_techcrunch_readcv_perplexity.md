# Read.cv Shutdown & Perplexity Acquisition
**Source:** TechCrunch + HN #42742241 + aibase.com
**Date collected:** 20260526
**Citation IDs:** V100, V101

---

## TL;DR (150-250 words)
Read.cv was a minimalist professional networking platform founded in 2021 by Andy Chung (ex-Facebook, Mozilla, Quip) that positioned itself as a design-forward alternative to LinkedIn. In January 2025, Perplexity AI acquired the company in an acqui-hire, absorbing its three-person team primarily for their expertise in consumer and social product design. The platform itself was wound down: users had until May 16, 2025 to export their data, and the ".cv" domain infrastructure was handed off to Hello.cv. The acquisition was described by Chung as "incredibly bittersweet." From the Hacker News community reaction, the failure mode was classic: a beloved small platform with a design-conscious community could not scale to profitability, and its founders found a more sustainable landing pad inside a well-funded AI company. The product had added a "Sites" feature allowing users to create personal websites with custom ".cv" domains — an attempt to monetize — but wasn't enough. Read.cv represents the recurring tragedy of "profile-forward" professional platforms: they attract early adopters who care about design and curation, but can't convert that love into durable network effects or revenue.

**Acquirer:** Perplexity AI

## What the Product Was
- Professional social network competing with LinkedIn
- Minimalist, design-focused profile pages
- Posts/feed (Posts.cv product)
- Job listings and candidate search
- "Sites" feature: personal websites with custom .cv domains

## Founder Quote
Andy Chung described the acquisition as "incredibly bittersweet," saying the team believes "great things happen when the world's knowledge is made more open and accessible."

## Perplexity CEO Quote
Aravind Srinivas: "The team is incredibly capable in designing and building consumer and social experiences and we look forward to working with them on many new exciting directions!"

## Why It Matters for This Research
Read.cv tried to solve the exact problem our product is targeting: professionals want a design-forward, curated professional identity page. The platform's failure reveals:
1. Even with strong design and a passionate community, professional networks need critical mass to sustain themselves
2. The "profile aggregation" model is hard to monetize — Read.cv's Sites feature was likely the clearest revenue path, but came too late
3. Acqui-hires are the exit for failed-but-beloved professional identity tools

## HN Community Reaction (V101)
**Top themes from Hacker News comments:**
- "Users help grow a product with their network, their content, or their money, get comfortable with that product, integrate it into their lives... and then it's yanked out." (common refrain about centralized platforms)
- Appreciation for the product: "Finding and building a community for (tech) creatives is hard. Adding networking and post sharing aspects was stellar."
- Platform shutting down on "a rather aggressive schedule" — short notice for users
- Users pushed toward self-hosted alternatives: Reactive Resume, personal sites
- Pattern: beloved small professional platforms → acqui-hire → shutdown

## Strategic Note
Read.cv offered something different: it let professionals build an identity layer *on top of* LinkedIn without replacing it. Users used Read.cv as their "beautiful public face" while LinkedIn remained the professional record. The failure suggests the gap is real but the monetization path is unclear.
