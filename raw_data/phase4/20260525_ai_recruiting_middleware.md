# AI Recruiting Middleware — SeekOut / Eightfold / Findem (LinkedIn Data Dependency)

## Why this matters
These three companies represent the **"middleware" layer between candidate data and recruiter workflow**. They tell us:
1. How much LinkedIn data is being scraped/used by the AI recruiting ecosystem (a lot)
2. Whether there's an existing buyer market for **enriched candidate data** (yes)
3. Where the legal/regulatory frontier is (Eightfold lawsuit 2025)

## SeekOut
- "Aggregates over **600-750M candidate profiles**" — some recent sources say **over 1B**
- Sources: LinkedIn, GitHub, research papers, patents, social media
- Positioning: "Agentic AI Recruiting Platform | Source, Screen & Engage Talent"
- Building toward agentic recruiter — direct LinkedIn competitor in some workflows

## Eightfold
- **"Proprietary global data set — world's largest, self-refreshing source of talent data"** — profiles of **1B+ workers**
- Sources: career sites, job boards, LinkedIn
- **2025: Proposed class-action lawsuit** alleging Eightfold uses LinkedIn data + job boards + applicant internet activity to assess success likelihood
- This is the legal frontier — the "is unauthorized aggregation of LinkedIn data OK?" question is now in court

## Findem
- Continuously ingests/refreshes **1.6 trillion public data points** from 100,000+ sources
- Sources: GitHub, ResearchGate, LinkedIn, and many more
- Positioning: AI talent intelligence with deeper "person-level" insights than competitors
- Sells to enterprise (Fortune 500 hiring teams)

## Common thread
- **All three depend on LinkedIn as primary spine**
- They differentiate by aggregating ADJACENT data (GitHub, papers, social, internet behavior)
- None of them build a **candidate-facing identity product** — they're recruiter-side only
- All bet that the **"AI sourcing better than human sourcing"** thesis pays out at enterprise scale

## What this tells us about our product positioning

### The middleware layer is the natural B-end customer
- These companies pay real money for enriched candidate data
- If we can produce **better signal on AI-using professionals than LinkedIn**, we have natural buyers
- Note: NOT head-to-head competition with LinkedIn Hiring Assistant; we'd be selling enrichment data UPSTREAM to SeekOut/Findem/Eightfold (or directly to enterprise) — similar to Handshake's "training data for AI labs" pivot

### The legal frontier matters
- Eightfold lawsuit signals that unauthorized scraping of LinkedIn for training/inference is now legally risky
- A product where users **explicitly consent** to data being used for B-end matching has a regulatory advantage
- The "user-authorized data flywheel" angle is increasingly valuable AS AI scraping faces legal heat

### The cross-platform gap
- All three middleware players scrape LinkedIn + GitHub + papers + patents + sometimes social
- None of them have **first-party access** to the candidate
- A C-end product that explicitly aggregates "your work everywhere" with user consent → first-party data → better quality than scraped
