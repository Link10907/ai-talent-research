# Doximity — The Successful Vertical Professional Network

## Primary facts (FY2025-2026)
- Founded 2010 (Jeff Tangney, ex-Epocrates)
- **3M+ registered members** — covers **85% of ~1M US physicians**, plus large portions of NPs, PAs, medical students
- Q2 FY2026: **650,000 prescribers used workflow tools** (record)
- IPO 2021 (NYSE: DOCS)

## Revenue (the success story)
- FY2025 total revenue: **$570.4M** (+20% YoY)
- FY2026 guidance: **$640-$646M**
- Q2 FY2026 revenue: $168.5M (+23% YoY)
- Growth deceleration: 20% in FY2025 → 13% projected FY2026 (vertical saturating)

## Business model
- B2B: Marketing, Hiring, Workflow Solutions sold to **pharma companies and health systems**
- Recently launched **Clinical AI Suite** (Ask, Scribe, Dialer) — adopted across 140+ US health systems
- Note: Doctors use it for FREE; revenue comes from pharma marketing + health system enterprise + recruiting

## Why Doximity worked where Polywork/Read.cv didn't

### Structural advantages
1. **Hard credential gate**: NPI number / state license verification at signup — instant trust layer, no AI slop possible
2. **Built-in B-end demand**: Pharma reps and health systems desperately need physician attention/sourcing; willingness to pay is multiples higher than ordinary recruiter market
3. **Compliance moat**: HIPAA-grade Dialer for telemedicine → workflow lock-in beyond "social" use
4. **Total addressable user count is finite and known**: ~1M US physicians → once you have 85%, you've effectively saturated the supply side, eliminating cold start indefinitely
5. **Regulatory exclusion of competitors**: LinkedIn can't easily build HIPAA-compliant tools; general-purpose networks can't get physician adoption at this density

### What it shows about niche identity products
- A vertical professional network CAN compete with LinkedIn if:
  - The vertical has hard credentials (license, certification)
  - B-end pays disproportionately (regulated industries, narrow ICP)
  - Workflow features can be built into the product (not just social)
  - User count is finite but high-density coverage is achievable

## Implication for our research
- Doximity is the **rare example** of a sustainable LinkedIn alternative
- Its success is **NOT replicable for "AI-using knowledge workers"** because:
  - No credential gate (anyone can claim to "use AI")
  - No clearly defined ICP for B-end
  - No regulatory moat
- BUT the lesson is: **find a hard verifiable signal of the user's value**, and the B-end will pay enormously
- For our product: **"verified portfolio with provenance"** (GitHub commits, published work, real B-end project links) could be a softer version of the Doximity license
