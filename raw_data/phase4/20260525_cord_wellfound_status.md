# Cord & Wellfound — 2024-2026 Status

## Cord (London-based UK tech hiring platform)

### Primary facts
- London-based recruitment platform, B2B SaaS hiring tool
- Indexes **5,000+ UK tech companies**, ranks by headcount growth (12-month rolling)
- Subcategories: FinTech, AI, Data & Analytics, E-commerce & D2C, InsurTech, Security, HealthTech, Sustainability, Blockchain, Consultancy
- Trustpilot: 3,034 customer reviews as of March 2025
- Sells to companies — direct person-to-person messaging, high response rates as USP
- Mostly positive Trustpilot tone — clear job post structure, transparent interview stages
- Capterra UK 2025 listing, G2 reviews active
- LinkedIn page active

### What Cord IS
- A recruiter sourcing tool, not a consumer professional network
- "Active candidates" pre-qualified pipeline
- More tool than network — closer to Lever/Greenhouse than LinkedIn
- UK-only scope is its niche

### Why it's not a real "LinkedIn replacement"
- B2B tool for employers, not C-end identity product
- Limited geography (UK only)
- No public signal of explosive growth or breakout funding round in 2024-2026
- Quiet, niche, sustainable — but not "the next LinkedIn"

## Wellfound (formerly AngelList Talent)

### Primary facts
- Spun out of AngelList November 2022 (CEO Amit Matani)
- Claims: 130K+ active job listings, 35K+ companies, 10M+ registered job seeker profiles
- **Android app removed from Google Play Store March 8, 2024**
- No iOS job-search app — browser-only as of 2026

### "Graveyard" pattern (user reviews, Trustpilot Sept 2024-2026)
- "Used to be a good place to find employment but has started feeling more and more like a **graveyard**"
- "Companies are now updating their listings far less frequently"
- "**80% of applications expire unseen** — it's an invisible confidence killer"
- "Listings that exist for **investor optics**, not to hire anyone" (fake / stale postings)
- Salary filter design flaw: turning it on hides all listings that don't include salary range — eliminating most of the catalog

### Death mechanism
1. **Brand reset (AngelList → Wellfound 2022) decoupled it from the AngelList parent's network strength** — Wellfound stands alone, AngelList directs investor/cap-table flow elsewhere
2. **B-side neglect**: Job postings expire, companies don't refresh, recruiter side stops investing → candidates apply into a "black hole"
3. **No mobile presence**: Killed Android app, never built iOS — in 2026 job search is mobile-first for most candidates
4. **Trust collapse from listing staleness**: Once candidates learn applications "expire unseen", they stop applying, which makes the platform feel even more dead — death spiral
5. **No clear AI strategy or refounding moment** comparable to Handshake's PhD pivot

### Why this matters for our research
- Demonstrates that **even with 10M profiles**, a job board can decay if B-side liquidity isn't maintained
- Confirms that "infrastructure" without active flywheel becomes graveyard — relevant to any identity product that promises eventual B-side payoff
- Suggests there is now an **abandoned niche audience** (startup job seekers, early-stage builders) that no one is serving well
