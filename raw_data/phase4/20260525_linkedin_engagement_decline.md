# LinkedIn Engagement Decline 2024-2025 — The Vulnerability Window

## The numbers
- **Average time on LinkedIn dropped from 58 min (2019) → ~30 min (2024-2025)** in the US (Schaefer Marketing Solutions / industry data)
- 2025 study across ~400,000 profiles (Q4 2025 algorithm reset analysis):
  - **Average visibility dropped ~47%**
  - **Engagement dropped 39%**
  - **Follower growth dropped 42%**
  - **Median impressions down 65% since 2023**

## User sentiment
- "Active engagement — sharing updates and interacting in comments — has given way to passive browsing"
- "Users are growing tired of inauthentic, repetitive, and self-promotional content"
- "Over-reliance on AI may ultimately alienate people in networks instead of leading to higher engagement"
- The "feels artificial" complaint is structural, not just aesthetic

## LinkedIn's response
- "AI solving AI" detection systems — suppress generic posts, bot comments, engagement bait
- Posts flagged as low-quality not removed but distribution-suppressed
- **Engagement pods (artificial mutual-liking groups) being dismantled** algorithmically
- Q4 2025 algorithm reset specifically targeted AI slop

## Strategic interpretation

### The vulnerability
- Half of long-form content is AI; engagement is down 39-65%; time-on-platform halved over 5 years
- LinkedIn's core engagement metric (the feed) is **structurally compromised**
- Users are still THERE (1B+) but they're **disengaging** from posting/interacting

### Why LinkedIn doesn't lose the war
- **Profile remains canonical** — even if engagement is dead, people maintain profiles for inbound recruiter contact
- **Recruiter side is paying** — that's the revenue, not consumer engagement
- **Feed decline doesn't directly threaten Hiring Assistant or Premium Career** — these don't depend on feed engagement
- They can lose half the engagement and still extract more revenue per user

### Where the window opens
- **Output-driven identity surface** that doesn't depend on posting in a corporate feed
- An identity product where the "content" is **work artifacts** (auto-aggregated) not deliberate posts
- Possibly a "feed" that's outputs from your work, not opinions about work

## Implication for our research
- The slop crisis IS real, but LinkedIn's response (verification + suppression) is rapid
- The window for an alternative is **narrower than the engagement numbers suggest** because LinkedIn doesn't need engagement to dominate
- Don't position around "feed quality" — position around **what LinkedIn structurally can't do** (auto-aggregated work artifacts, AI-augmented capability proof, etc.)
