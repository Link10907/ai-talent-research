# Phase 3 Legal — Key findings raw notes (2026-05-25)

## Adoption (individual vs firm gap)
- 79% of legal professionals say they use AI in some capacity at their firm (Clio 2024 cited multiple times).
- 69% of legal professionals personally use GenAI for work (8am Report 2026); 28% daily, 31% several times/week. Up from 31% in 2025 and 27% in 2024 — more than doubled YoY.
- 46% of firms have implemented general-purpose AI; only 34% have adopted legal-specific tools.
- Large firms 87% AI use; solo firms 71% (Clio 2025).
- Mid/big BigLaw (51+): 39% GenAI adoption. Firms <50 lawyers: ~20%. (AffiniPay/MyCase 2025).
- Only 28% of firms say they currently use GenAI; only 15% say AI is "central" to workflow (Juris Digital interpretation).
- 53% of legal professionals say their firm has no AI policy or they are unaware of one.

## Firm size split
- 87% adoption at large firms vs 71% at solo (Clio).
- Solo/small firms: gen AI adoption nearly doubled to 53% (Clio Solo & Small Firm 2025).
- ChatGPT (66%), Microsoft Copilot (42%), Gemini (24%) for small firms — mostly consumer-grade.
- Only 4% of small firms have adopted AI "widely or universally."
- Fewer than 33% of solo/small firms have grown revenue with AI vs ~60% of enterprise.
- 86% of solo, 78% of small firms have made no pricing changes due to AI.

## In-house counsel
- 52% of US in-house counsel started using GenAI in 2025 — more than double YoY (2026 ACC CLO Survey).
- 90%+ of lawyers now use at least one AI tool in daily work (some sources).
- 14% reduction in outside counsel spend = ~$252K savings for median legal dept ($1.8M outside counsel spend).
- CLOC 2025: 63% of legal depts cite workload/bandwidth as top challenge; 83% expect demand to grow.

## Tools and benchmarks
- Harvey: 1,000+ customers in 60 countries; 50% of Am Law 100; CMS rolled out firmwide to 7,000+ lawyers across 50+ countries; 2/3 of customers report measurable benefit within 90 days; pricing $50K-$100K+/year/firmwide.
- Harvey benchmark (Vals 2025): 94.8% accuracy on document Q&A — top scores in 5 of 6 tasks.
- CoCounsel (Thomson Reuters): 89.6% on document Q&A; 79.5% average; integrated into Westlaw. Casetext shut down as standalone April 1, 2025.
- Eve: $103M Series B at $1B+ valuation; 450+ plaintiff firms; processes 200K+ cases/year.
- Spellbook: $50M Series B led by Khosla (Rabois); 3,800-4,000 legal teams in 80 countries; transactional/contract review in Word; uses GPT-5/Claude.
- Ironclad: 75% reduction in contracting time, 70% cost reduction in case studies.
- Evisort: 238+ pretrained clause types.
- 74% of GC/leadership deploying or planning GenAI.

## Sub-role adoption depth
Per LawSites 8am Report:
- Paralegals = #1 group targeted for AI training (39%)
- Partners (30%)
- Associates (26%)
Common applications: Drafting correspondence (58%), Legal research (58%), Document summarization (47%), Document drafting (43-49%).

## BigLaw training initiatives
- Ropes & Gray "TrAIlblazers": first-year associates may allocate up to 400 of their 1,900 annual billable hours to AI training/experimentation (20%).
- Latham & Watkins: mandatory 2-day AI Academy in DC for all 400 first-years (2024 & 2025), focused on Harvey + Copilot.
- A&O Shearman + Harvey developed AI agents jointly.
- Paul Weiss embedded Harvey starting 2023.
- Clifford Chance cut London jobs citing AI adoption.

## Hiring pressure
- Major firms have "reduced the pace" of associate hiring / cut summer associate programs (2025 market reports).
- "Students with AI skills are becoming 'the more attractive candidates'; those without will be left behind."
- Legal engineer / legal technologist now a "fast-growing career path."
- 65% of firms hiring legal tech experts over traditional attorneys (per JDJournal — likely high-water claim, treat with caution).

## Job-postings — explicit AI requirements
- AI Legal Counsel: median $139,438; range $84K–$197K (ZipRecruiter May 2026).
- AI Governance Counsel: median $158,750; senior $273K+ (IAPP / Axial Search).
- Latham & Watkins published a "Senior Manager of AI Governance" JD.
- Responsibilities: drafting AI-specific contracts, advising on data privacy/IP for AI, ensuring compliance with EU AI Act / state-level AI laws, developing internal training, leading AI governance frameworks.
- AI governance had the highest YoY salary growth of any privacy-adjacent specialty.

## How lawyers showcase AI ability
- LinkedIn primary channel: 87% of lawyers on it; Featured section = de facto portfolio.
- LawJobs.com guidance: "call out AI skillset, link to articles or speaking engagements related to AI adoption."
- Resume "Tech Skills" sections listing Harvey, CoCounsel, Copilot, Westlaw AI, RelativityOne, Clio Duo, NetDocuments AI.
- LinkedIn published articles, speaking at ILTACON/Legalweek, CLE talks as visible signals.
- Prompt engineering certifications (Vanderbilt Coursera; Clio Legal AI Fundamentals — free) cited as resume-able.
- No legal-specific portfolio platform exists.

## Resistance / ethical guardrails (the countervailing force)
- ABA Formal Opinion 512 (July 29, 2024) — first formal ethics guidance. Covers competence, confidentiality, communication, candor toward tribunal, supervisory responsibilities, fees. Cannot bill clients for time learning a general AI tool.
- Hallucination sanctions: 600+ tracked cases by spring 2025. Rate up from 2/week to 2-3/day (Charlotin database).
- Mostafavi (CA, 2025): 21 of 23 case quotations fabricated → record-setting state fine.
- Johnson v. Dunn (N.D. Ala. July 2025): large firm disqualified from case; published opinion; clerk informed bar regulators in each state.
- Morgan & Morgan (one of largest US plaintiff firms): order to show cause for ChatGPT fail.
- Lindell ($3K/each fine), MyPillow.
- Confidentiality / privacy concerns are #1 roadblock for 50% of legal pros.
- Billable hour viewed as economically threatened — but Bloomberg Law also: "AI Boosts Legal Productivity Without Toppling Billable Hours" — split views.
- 59% of firms used flat fees in 2024 (alongside or instead of hourly).

## Market context

- US lawyers: ~1.37M (up from 1.35M in 2024).
- US law firms: ~463,590.
- Large firms = 38% of market share; small (2-10) = 34%.
