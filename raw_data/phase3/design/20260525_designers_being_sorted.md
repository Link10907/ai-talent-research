# Bootcamp — "Product Designers Are Not Being Replaced. They Are Being Sorted."

Source: https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/product-designers-are-not-being-replaced-they-are-being-sorted-6e2fb0492ed2

## Core thesis
AI bifurcates the design field — not broad replacement but a sort into adapters vs displaced.

## Hiring data points
- "More than half say they are primarily hiring for senior roles, while only around a quarter are filling junior positions"
- "73% of hiring managers want candidates proficient in AI tools"
- 91% of designers using AI report it improves quality
- Per Jenny Wen, three hiring archetypes emerging:
  1. Senior strategists
  2. Code-adjacent builders ("designer-engineers")
  3. "Cracked new grads" without legacy process baggage

## Quote
"Your value was never in pushing rectangles. AI just forced everyone to see that."

## New required skills
1. Strategic judgment
2. AI literacy (prompt eng + output curation)
3. Systems thinking (clean component naming + tokens — machine-readable)
4. Hybrid capabilities — AX (Agent Experience), MX (Machine Experience)

## Implication for product
"Show me what you can do with AI in your workflow" is becoming a hiring requirement — but the artifact format for proving this doesn't exist yet (portfolio = static images, can't capture prompt workflow + iteration).
