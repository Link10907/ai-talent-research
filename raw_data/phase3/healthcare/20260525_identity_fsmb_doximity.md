# Physician Professional Identity Infrastructure (2025-2026)

## ABMS — board certification = canonical professional identity

- ABMS Member Boards: 24 certifying boards
- Nearly 1 million active board-certified physicians
- 38 medical specialties + 89 subspecialties
- Central, daily-updated database of all certified physicians
- "Is My Doctor Certified?" public lookup at certificationmatters.org
- This is the *de facto* canonical professional identity for US physicians. **Any "professional identity product" for physicians will live in the shadow of ABMS certification**, which is the only credential that matters for licensure, hospital privileges, and insurance paneling.
- International parallels: GMC (UK), RACP/RACGP (Australia/NZ), CFPC (Canada)

## Doximity — incumbent "LinkedIn for doctors"

- Reaches 80%+ of US physicians (verified members)
- 50% of NPs/PAs verified
- HIPAA-compliant messaging
- Doximity Talent Finder for recruiters at ~$12K/seat/year
- Newsfeed of curated medical research, journal publications
- Telehealth (Doximity Dialer)
- Members can take consulting work at avg $375/hour through the platform
- IPO'd June 2021. Highly profitable; ~9-10x revenue multiples even after 2022 correction
- **Bob Ambrogi & Healthcare Success commentary:** "Doximity is not the new LinkedIn." Different use cases — Doximity for clinical reach, referral, internal credibility; LinkedIn for cross-industry visibility, career mobility outside clinical practice.

## FSMB (Federation of State Medical Boards) — April 2024 AI guidelines

The 70 state-and-territory medical boards aligned around these principles:
1. **Transparency and Accountability:** Physicians must be proficient in the AI tools they use, understand how they function and on what data they were trained.
2. **Disclosure:** Doctors should be transparent about AI use to patients and able to explain how it works.
3. **Human Decision-Making:** Chatbots cannot stand in for doctors.
4. **Risk-Based Regulation:** Not all AI carries same risk; accountability scaled to patient risk.

**Critical takeaway:** "Doctors are responsible for their use of AI and accountable for any harm." In disciplinary proceedings, boards will assess source of error (data, algorithm, or physician judgment).

**Implications for "showing off AI ability":** A physician publicly listing "uses ChatGPT for diagnosis" exposes themselves to liability narrative. Showcasing AI proficiency carries career risk that doesn't exist in marketing/design.

## SoMeDocs, KevinMD, MedFluencers — physician personal brand ecosystem

- **KevinMD (Kevin Pho):** since 2004, ~50K newsletter, large LinkedIn following. Mantra: "If you don't define your brand, others will."
- **SoMeDocs (Dr. Dana Corriel):** physician-turned-entrepreneur, builds physician personal brands on LinkedIn and beyond. Specifically targets *non-AI* brand building.
- **MedFluencers:** physician-led influencer agency, brand deals.
- **Sermo:** physician network, paid surveys, side hustle income. Not identity-focused.
- **Pattern:** physician personal brand market exists, but the activity centers on (1) thought leadership in clinical specialty, (2) wellness/lifestyle/entrepreneurship, NOT (3) AI proficiency. AI is a tool, not an identity.

## TikTok Clinician Creator Network (UK)

- 19 NHS-qualified clinicians launched into formal network with 2.2M followers combined.
- Distinct from "AI doctor" deepfake problem on TikTok (multiple AI-generated fake doctor videos in 2025-2026 prompted FDA warnings).
- Signal: legitimate "verified clinician creator" channels are a growing niche, with platforms (TikTok, Doximity) trying to formalize them.

## Sub-group identity comparison

| Sub-group | Identity infrastructure | Showing off AI helps? |
|---|---|---|
| Clinician (MD/DO) | ABMS + state license + Doximity | Mixed: helps with side hustles, neutral-to-harmful with patients & boards |
| Clinical researcher (PhD or MD-PhD) | Publications + h-index + ORCID + ResearchGate | Yes — accelerates output |
| Nurse / NP | State BON + AANP / ANCC | Limited — workflow is institutional |
| Health informaticist | AMIA, HIMSS, board certs (CPHI, CHDA, CAHIMS, CPHIMS) | Strongly yes — this IS their identity |
| Digital health startup employee | LinkedIn / GitHub | Yes — same as software industry |
| Health content creator | YouTube/IG/TikTok/Substack | Mixed — audience expects authority |
| Aesthetic medicine / med spa | Instagram, Google reviews, before/after photos | Limited — visual outcomes dominate |
| Mental health practitioner | License + Psychology Today + insurance panel | Limited / regulated |
