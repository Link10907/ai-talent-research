# AMA + Doximity Physician AI Surveys (2025-2026)

## AMA 2026 Physician Survey on Augmented Intelligence (March 2026, ~1,700 physicians)

- **81%** of physicians use AI professionally (vs 38% in 2023 → 66% in 2024 → 72% in 2025 → 81% in 2026).
- Average **2.3 use cases** per physician.
- **39%**: summaries of medical research / standards of care
- **30%**: discharge instructions / care plans / progress notes
- **86%** rate data privacy as critical
- **88%** require robust safety / efficacy validation
- **88%** worried about AI-related skill loss in trainees
- **70%** very/somewhat concerned about skill loss in residents/students
- **85%** want to be consulted/involved in AI adoption decisions
- Clear liability frameworks rank #1 regulatory priority
- Source: ama-assn.org/practice-management/digital-health/more-80-physicians-use-ai-professionally-ama-survey (Feb 2026 publication of Q4 2025 data)

## Doximity 2026 State of AI in Medicine (3,151 US physicians)

- 94% use or want to use AI; only 5% no interest
- Adoption: 47% (Mar-Apr 2025) → 63% (Nov 2025-Jan 2026) → 54% "currently using" in clinical practice
- Daily use among AI users: 64% → 74%
- Top use cases:
  - Literature search: 35% (from 22%)
  - Voice-based documentation: 29% (from 20%)
  - Writing/admin tasks: growing
  - Insurance correspondence
- By specialty: Neurology 64% / Gastro 61% / Internal Med 60% / Family Med + Cardio 58% / Oncology 57%
- Demographics:
  - Men 57% / Women 49%
  - Under 30: 61% / 50s: 55% / 60+: lower but only 11% uninterested
- Benefits reported: 75% reduced admin / 75% job satisfaction / 69% better patient care / 49% capacity for new patients
- **71% cite "accuracy and reliability of AI outputs" as top concern** (78% in pediatrics)
- 88% believe AI can reduce burnout
- 90% see potential to reduce pajama time (est. 48% weekly reduction)
- Time saved: 75% personal / 72% wellness / 37% current patients / 19% new patients
- 47% report institutional AI policy "still evolving"
- Only 8% report clear understanding of policies

## Athenahealth 2025 survey (via Medical Economics, fetch blocked but snippet captured)

- Physicians are now using AI for clinical decision support, not just paperwork.
- Documentation drift: paperwork still #1 but clinical reasoning use cases growing fast.

## Pattern across all three surveys

Rapid YoY growth, but:
1. Individual usage outpaces institutional deployment
2. Concerns shift from "is it accurate?" to "who's liable?" as use deepens
3. Pediatricians & high-acuity specialties are most cautious
4. The single dominant use case is administrative (documentation) — clinical decision support is growing but still secondary
