# Fortune — Junior analysts beware: Wall Street jobs may be taken by AI (2025-06-02)

URL: https://fortune.com/2025/06/02/junior-analysts-wall-street-jobs-taken-by-ai/

## Key data
- Big firms considering pulling back hiring **up to two-thirds** as AI reliance grows
- Goldman Sachs partners told to identify roles "that could be made more efficient if replaced by AI"
- Entry-level IB analyst pay can reach $128k annually (Glassdoor)
- Typical analyst hours: 80-100/week
- Goldman headcount actually GREW: 48,300 staffers in Sept 2025, up 1,800 YoY — so layoff narrative > reality (so far)

## Named quotes
- **Christoph Rabenseifner** (Deutsche Bank CSO for Tech/Data/Innovation):
  > "The easy idea is you just replace juniors with an AI tool"
  But notes humans remain essential for decision-making.

- **Michael Ashley Schulman** (Running Point Capital Advisors, ex-Deutsche Bank VP):
  AI won't eliminate roles entirely but will "reduce the number of heads required to accomplish the same task." Predicts shift from data gathering → data verification + deeper analysis.

- **Jeanne Branthover** (DHR Global, Head of Global FS, 40yr veteran advising JPM, BNY Mellon):
  > "It's like being in a fraternity and being a pledge. You gotta put in the work"
  Apprenticeship-model defense of analyst program.

- **Tim Bates** (U-Michigan Prof, ex-Lenovo/GM CTO):
  AI is "reshaping" not replacing.

## Interpretation
Establishment voices say AI not a wholesale takeover. Junior analyst role becomes **AI supervision + judgment + interpretation** rather than data gathering.
