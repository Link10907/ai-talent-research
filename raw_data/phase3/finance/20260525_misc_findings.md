# Miscellaneous synthesized findings

## Goldman / JPMorgan in-house AI rollouts
- **Goldman Sachs**: GS AI Assistant rolled out firmwide mid-2025 after pilot with ~10,000 employees; model-agnostic (GPT, Gemini, Claude) in audited env. Used for doc summarization, drafting, data analysis, research translation.
- **JPMorgan**: Multiple IB CoPilots automating draft generation, pitchbook compilation, data gathering. JPM Virtual Assistant fields queries like "What wires cleared yesterday?" "Track cross-border Asia client transactions."
- Public ChatGPT BANNED across JPM, DB, WF, BofA, Citi, GS, MS — only sanctioned enterprise versions allowed.
- Quote (industry): "A first-year analyst can now supervise AI to do work that once required three analysts."

## BloombergGPT & analyst LLM adoption
- BloombergGPT integrations cut average analyst query time **20-30%** in 2024 pilots
- **38%** of financial analysts using LLMs for earnings summaries & forecasting
- **60%** of BofA clients using LLM-based investment/retirement planning tools
- Caveat: GPT-4 outperforms BloombergGPT on sentiment, headline classification, financial reasoning — so finance-specific LLMs not necessarily winning

## Buy-side / equity research
- **85%** of asset managers using AI somewhere
- Only **25%** of buy-side traders considering AI for actual equity trading
- AI reduces research costs **40%** while expanding coverage
- "Hybrid skill set" now required: traditional finance + data science + tech fluency

## PE / VC adoption (deep)
- **95%** of PE/VC firms use AI in some capacity
- **82%** for deal sourcing research
- Harmonic hit $1.45B valuation 2025 as early-stage sourcing platform
- SourceScrub dominates lower-middle market with proprietary AI deal sourcing
- Bessemer reclaimed **234 hours/analyst/year**

## Hedge fund AI hiring (Hedgeweek/Selby Jennings)
- Top quant & multi-strat funds aggressively poached talent from Google DeepMind, OpenAI over 18 months
- ML candidate market now SATURATED — but core engineering thinning
- New hybrid roles emerging: prompt engineering + RAG specialists
- Demand for AI-fluent quants outpaces supply, esp NY/London/Amsterdam
- Cross-functional squads (quant researcher + SWE + ML + PM) replacing siloed roles

## Prompt engineer in finance
- JPMorgan prompt engineer: **$110-220k** range
- Goldman prompt engineer: **$115-235k** range
- Project Pluto Financial Prompt Engineer (real listing) requires 1-4yr front-office at top FS firms, financial research/modeling, GenAI tools
- Most listings prefer 2+ yrs in IB/PE/HF + demonstrated AI tool use

## CFO survey data
- **87%** CFOs say AI will be extremely/very important to finance ops in 2026
- AI adoption in finance: **59%** (2025) vs **58%** (2024) — adoption rate flatlining at the function level
- **35%** of finance teams use ChatGPT
- **75%** of CFOs worry security/privacy
- **35%** say data trust is top barrier to AI ROI, but only **10%** fully trust enterprise data
- Few CFOs see substantial AI ROI yet

## AI-native finance startups
- **Earthian AI**: First "AI-native hedge fund" — autonomous reasoning models replace static quant rules
- **Altbridge AI**: Fully autonomous investing, no humans in investment loop (ex-SWF CIO + IMO gold medalist founders)
- **Numerai**: $30M raise 2025; J.P. Morgan provided up to $500M capacity; growing toward $1B AUM
- **YC Spring 2026 RFS**: explicitly calls for "AI-native hedge fund platforms generating alpha via coordinated AI agent swarms"

## MBA finance hiring shift
- Workers w/ AI skills earn **56%** more than peers (up from 25% premium just 1yr ago)
- ~25% of employers say AI proficiency now BASELINE for MBA-level roles
- BUT: prestigious pedigree still dominates — 40% BB hires from Ivy+; first-round invites 60-70% target schools vs 20-30% non-target
- Banks said to be looking for "top 0.1%" not top 1% if associate seats shrink — pedigree filter HARDENS while AI skill becomes table stakes
