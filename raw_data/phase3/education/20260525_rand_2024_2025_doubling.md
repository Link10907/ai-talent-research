# RAND ASDP & teacher surveys (synthesized from search results)
Sources: rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RRA4180-1; RRA956-31; RRA4412-2; RRA134-25 (2024)

## Headline doubling
- ELA/Math/Science teachers using AI for school: 25% (2024) -> 53% (2025) — doubling YoY
- Student K-12 use for school: also ~54% (Sept 2025 RAND)
- This includes "any use" — not weekly

## Subgroup variation
- Pre-K teachers: only 29% used GenAI in 2024-25 (RRA4412-2)
- High school teachers and STEM teachers lead in adoption
- Title-I / lower-income districts trail
- Charter schools and well-resourced suburban districts lead

## Training and policy gap
- Spring 2024: 23% of districts had trained teachers on AI -> Fall 2024: 48%
- By Fall 2025: ~74% of districts plan AI training
- BUT: 80%+ of students report teachers have NOT explicitly taught them how to use AI
- 71% of K-12 teachers say they have no AI training (one source); 43% have had at least one session (RAND, +50% from prior survey)

## RAND conclusion
"AI use in schools is quickly increasing but guidance lags behind" — explicit policy gap.

## Important nuance
The "AI is now used by half of teachers" stat masks: (a) most use is occasional, not workflow-integrated; (b) student use is for cheating in many cases; (c) policies are absent.
