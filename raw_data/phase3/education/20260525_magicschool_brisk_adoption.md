# MagicSchool + Brisk Teaching — adoption signals
Source: https://www.magicschool.ai/magicschool; humai.blog; edtechindex.org

## MagicSchool (synthesized)
- "More than 6 million educators use MagicSchool" as of Oct 2025 — exceeding total K-12 teachers in US (~3.7M public + ~500K private). Includes international, paraprofessionals, free trial users.
- Growth timeline: 1,500 in 3 wks (May 2023 launch) -> 2M in 4 months -> 4M by Jan 2024 -> 6M+ by Oct 2025
- 94% of teachers report 7+ hours/week saved (self-reported, on-site)
- 4 in 5 teachers report improved student engagement

## Pricing tiers
- Free: core tools, usage limits
- Plus: expanded features
- Enterprise: district-wide, SSO, dashboards (negotiated pricing)

## Features
- Lesson plan generator, rubric, presentation, quiz, worksheet, assessment, differentiation, communication, custom chatbots, report card comments
- Integrates with Google Workspace, Canvas, Clever, ClassLink, Schoology, Microsoft

## Brisk Teaching
- Browser extension model — works inside Google Docs/Slides + Microsoft 365
- 93% Common Sense privacy rating (highest among ed AI)
- Differentiates from MagicSchool by living inside teachers' existing tools

## Implication
The K-12 AI tool layer is MATURE. The dominant pattern: free + freemium, district sales for revenue.
Teachers themselves rarely pay; districts pay or teachers use free tier.
Direct C-side monetization of K-12 teachers is structurally difficult — but they are reachable through
free tools and adjacent network channels.
