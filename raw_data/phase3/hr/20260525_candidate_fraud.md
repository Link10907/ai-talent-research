# Candidate Fraud / Identity Verification — Compiled

Sources:
- https://staffinghub.com/hiring/candidate-verification-ai-resume-fraud-staffing-firms-2026/
- https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/blog/microsoft-security-blog/fake-employees-real-threat-decentralized-identity-to-combat-deepfake-hiring/4479864
- Insight Global 2025 AI in Hiring report
- HR Dive Checkr survey

## Scale
- FTC job-scam losses: $90M (2020) → $501M (2024). +457% in 4 yrs.
- FBI IC3 2024: 20,044 employment-fraud complaints, $264M losses, +276% YoY.
- LinkedIn: 74% of all social-media job scams target LinkedIn.
- Gartner projection: 1-in-4 candidate profiles will be partially or fully FAKE by 2028.

## On the candidate side
- 70%+ recruiters have encountered AI-generated resumes/portfolios.
- 90% HR workers report increased AI-generated applications in 2025.
- 30%+ hiring mgrs interviewed someone later found to have fake identity.
- 33%+ saw a substitute candidate participate in the interview.

## On the employer side
- Only 19% hiring mgrs "extremely confident" their process catches identity fraud.
- One cybersecurity firm: 23.2% of all applicants flagged as fraud risk over 3-month window.
- 23% of businesses lost >$50K from a fraudulent placement; 10% >$100K.
- 39% hiring mgrs now do MORE in-person interviews to fight AI noise.
- Amazon: blocked 1,800+ suspected North Korean scammers since Apr 2024, volume +27% per quarter.

## Recommended solution
- Move identity verification EARLIER (assessment stage) rather than late-stage background check.
- Microsoft Entra Verified ID / decentralized identity for "good friction."

## Implication for our project
This is the strongest macro tailwind for the "real, verifiable identity" thesis. Buyer-side TA leaders are actively WILLING TO PAY for identity assurance. The B2B value of a verified-identity professional graph is rising fast — which feeds back into B2B monetization for our product. On C-side, candidates will increasingly NEED to differentiate from fake profiles, which gives us a reason for them to verify themselves.
