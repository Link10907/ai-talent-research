# I Hate It Here — HR's Perception Issue (Hebba Youssef, Feb 24 2025)

Source: https://hateithere.co/articles/2025-02-24/hrs-perception-issue/
Newsletter: 136K subscribers, audience = burned-out HR/People Ops practitioners.

## Identity / status anxiety
- "Ever tell someone you work in HR and their reaction and energy towards you just… changes?"
- HR seen as "paper pushers" rather than strategic partners
- Status anxiety = central concern; pros are denied "seats at decision-making tables"

## Burnout
- "A lot of HR teams spend more time fixing problems than focusing on proactive work."
- Reactive posture perpetuates administrative perception, traps practitioners in exhausting cycles.

## Identity reframing prescribed
- HR pros must demonstrate "impact on things like growth, revenue and innovation"
- Speak "the language of the C-suite"
- Connect initiatives to ROI

## AI mention
- Brief mention of "The Hive" — an AI search engine for HR pros, positioned as efficiency tool.
- AI not framed as identity threat; framed as upgrade.

## Implication for our project
HR pros are SIGNAL-HUNGRY for credibility / status / business impact. A platform that lets them showcase "I drove $X revenue / saved $Y / hired Z critical-role engineers" with verifiable outcomes would tap real demand. But they're loyal to LinkedIn and skeptical of newcomers — see "I Hate It Here" itself: it operates on Substack + LinkedIn + podcast, not a new platform.
