# Where & How Marketers Show AI Capability

## Existing portfolio culture (pre-AI)
- Strong: design-heavy marketing roles (brand, social media, content) use Behance, Dribbble, personal websites
- Strong for creators: TikTok/IG/YouTube acts AS portfolio
- Medium: copywriters publish samples on personal sites + Medium + LinkedIn
- Weak: performance marketers, growth marketers, demand gen, PMM, marketing ops historically don't show portfolios (mostly resumes + case study slides)
- Lovable guide notes: portfolios organized around "problem-solution-results" with case studies, not by workflow
- "Portfolios with quantified achievements generate 2.5x more interview callbacks"

## Showcasing AI specifically
LinkedIn:
- Update headline ("AI Marketing Specialist")
- Featured section as "digital portfolio" linking AI projects, prompt libraries, certificates
- Share thought leadership posts (prompts, workflows, hot takes)
- 340% increase in LinkedIn job postings requiring AI marketing skills (18 months)

X/Twitter:
- New models, prompts, tools, workflows are shared on X first
- Marketing AI KOLs: The Rundown (Rowan Cheung, 563K followers), Eddie Shleyner (60K+ newsletter), Kyle Poyar (84K Growth Unhinged readers), Emily Kramer (MKT1), Alex Velinov (popular prompt LinkedIn lists)
- Pattern: viral threads with prompts → newsletter subscriptions → consulting / job offers

Substacks / newsletters:
- Demand Curve, Reforge, Growth Unhinged, MKT1, Lenny's, Marketing AI Institute, Rundown AI
- One newsletter post can become 10+ social media pieces (cross-platform repurposing)

Prompt libraries:
- HubSpot, Siege Media (101 tested prompts), Notion templates, Juma/Team-GPT (100+ curated)
- Marketers build & share own libraries — emerging form of "AI portfolio"
- GitHub repo "coreyhaines31/marketingskills" shares CRO/copy/SEO/analytics/growth Claude skills

## Built artifacts as portfolio
- Vibe marketers ship Lovable/Bolt apps, internal tools, Zapier automations
- "Former growth lead who now builds tools with Gumloop or Lovable" — example
- Personal brand campaigns (newsletter, LinkedIn series) treated as "live case studies"

## What's MISSING in current behavior
- No unified place where marketers document their AI workflow / show "this is how I think with AI"
- Prompt libraries are scattered (Notion docs, Twitter threads, internal docs)
- Hard to verify AI capability from a resume — agencies report screening difficulty
- Junior marketers especially have nowhere to demonstrate AI proficiency without 5+ years experience

## User behavior data points
- 91.9% of creators use at least one AI tool in workflow (creator economy report)
- 70%+ of marketing roles require hands-on AI experience (cited by industry analyses)
- 25 startup marketing Claude skills libraries published by community (the-ai-corner.com)
- Agencies prefer Claude for customer-facing copy (fewer editing passes)
