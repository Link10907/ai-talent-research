# Marketing Displacement & Hiring Disruption 2025

## Adweek / Mark Ritson piece
- 65% of marketing tasks are replaceable by AI (Anthropic report)
- Marketing ranks 5th most vulnerable of 800 occupations
- US marketing job postings: -7% YoY, -15% QoQ in Q2 2025
- Hiring of 22-25 in AI-exposed roles: -14% vs 2022
- Marketing salaries flat in 2025, declining in real terms
- Voluntary quit rate: 2% (lowest in a decade)
- Most vulnerable functions:
  - Market research and analysis
  - Consumer research interpretation
  - Data analysis / competitive intel
  - Brand strategy components
  - Customer journey mapping
  - Forecasting / budgeting
  - Reports and presentations
- Ritson: "Junior analysts synthesizing research and building presentations face the greatest displacement risk"

## MarTech: AI's impact on early-career marketers
- Paul Roetzer (Marketing AI Institute):
  - "If you were just executing (building landing pages, writing copy, etc.), you're cooked. That is not a job one to two years out."
  - "AI is not just temporarily disrupting these roles; it's completely redefining them."
- BCG: 90% of marketing manager tasks disrupted from a skills perspective
- 16% relative employment decline among workers 22-25 post-ChatGPT
- Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei: 50% of entry-level white-collar jobs could disappear in 5 years

## CMI report - "Missing Generation"
- 1 in 3 marketers say org is reducing entry-level hiring
- Net hiring score: -19.8 points (reductions outpace increases 2.5x)
- 75% of employed marketers say finding jobs is "significantly or somewhat more challenging" than 2 years ago
- Avg job search: 5.2 months (up from 3.1 months in 2024)
- Fortune 500 wants "soft skills of storytelling, strategy, and critical thinking" — but only from 5+ years of experience
- Advice: "Build visible work demonstrating judgment rather than credentials alone"

## MarTech: AI threatens entry-level pipeline
- Tech cos cut 21-25 yo employees by 50% over 2 years
- Avg employee age at major tech: 34.3 → 39.4
- Recent grad unemployment (22-27): 5.8%, matching no-HS-diploma
- 75.8% identify AI expertise as major skills gap; only 5% actively building GenAI capabilities
- 43% wage premium for AI skills (up from 25% YoY)

## Layoff scale
- 100,000+ employees impacted by AI-driven layoffs in 2025
- 1/3 of CMOs expect to cut staff over next 1-2 years
- ~50% of marketing leaders at largest companies anticipate layoffs
- 69% of firms adopting GenAI marketing tools (2024-2025) fully terminated or substantially reduced external agency/BPO contracts within 18 months

## Upwork
- AI skills demand: +109% YoY in 2025
- Specific growth: AI video gen/edit +329%, AI integration +178%, AI image +95%
- 58% of businesses will prioritize AI proficiency when hiring freelancers
- Digital marketing demand remains stable / increasing
