# Parabola — State of AI in Operations 2025

URL: https://parabola.io/blog/introducing-the-state-of-ai-in-operations-2025-research-report
Date: Q3 2025
Sample: "Hundreds of operations professionals"; 89% directly involved in evaluating/implementing software & AI tools

## Headline statistics

### Adoption
- **98%** of operations teams are using or experimenting with AI
- Only **1.7%** not using AI at all
- 52% use AI in *some* workflows
- 36% are experimenting with small projects
- 10% have AI in most workflows OR as core operations

### Maturity self-assessment
- 45% intermediate (using AI for a few repeatable workflows)
- 21% advanced (AI creates competitive advantage)
- → Implication: the long tail of 79% is sub-advanced, opportunity for tooling/identity products that meet them where they are

### Top barriers (NOT budget)
- 41% trust & accuracy concerns
- 36% lack of expertise/training
- 9% cost/budget — i.e., money is not the bottleneck; competence is

### Budget direction
- 71% plan to increase AI budgets next 12 months
- 21% plan significant increase

### Highest-ROI use cases
- 41% data entry & cleaning
- 17% reporting
- 16% forecasting
- 13% reconciliation
- → Note: top use cases are still "automate the boring stuff," not "create new value"

### Industry standardization timeline
- 68% expect AI standard within 1-3 years
- 37% within 12 months
- 14% believe already standard

## Interpretation for product strategy
- Ops people self-report very high tool-touch but very low expertise.
  This is the perfect target for an AI-assisted *identity construction* product:
  they ARE using AI, they DON'T know how to talk about it, recruiters don't know
  how to evaluate it.
- Trust/accuracy concerns (41%) suggests they'd value a product that can *vet/verify*
  their AI usage credibly (i.e. "this person actually ships, here's the artifact").
