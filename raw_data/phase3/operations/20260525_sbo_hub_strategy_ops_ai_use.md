# SBO Hub (Vessela Clewley) — How Strategy & Ops Professionals Use AI

URL: https://sbohub.substack.com/p/how-strategy-and-ops-professionals-56a
Date: 2025
Sample: senior strategy & ops professionals (85% senior/exec-level; 70% Strategy/Planning, 45% RevOps)

## Tool adoption
- LLMs dominate: **ChatGPT/Claude at 46%**
- Traditional tools still essential: Google Suite/Sheets 24%, Slack 24%, Notion 22%
- **88% experimenting with AI** but only **44% fully integrated into workflows**
  — the "experiment-to-integration gap" is the gap our product could fill

## Specialized tools (real usage breakdown)
- Meeting productivity: Granola 23%, Fathom 18%
- Workflow automation: Lindy AI, Gumloop, Zapier Agents, n8n, make.com
- Data/analytics: Looker 26%, Tableau 17%
- Content/presentations: Gamma, Blotato
- Voice tools: Retell, WisprFlow
- Email management: Fyxer, Superhuman, Kairos

## Sociology of usage
- Users build **separate Projects/Collections per client** to reduce hallucinations
  and protect proprietary frameworks → already personalizing AI by domain context
- Quote: "Not one tool comes to mind as 'THE tool' as I switch between different tools
  based on use case." — fragmentation is structural
- "necessary evil due to time spent troubleshooting failures" — adoption with friction

## Top workflow improvement needs
1. Data Reporting & Analytics 17%
2. Account Mgmt / Revenue Ops 16%
3. Meeting Workflows 12%

## Interpretation
- These are heavy AI tinkerers building bespoke stacks — exactly the population that
  would benefit from a *canonical* way to surface their AI workflows/playbooks.
- The "client-segregated projects" behavior signals these people already think of
  their AI work as discrete *artifacts*. A product that lets them publish sanitized
  versions of those playbooks could land.
