# Sales Reps' Identity & Platform Behavior

## LinkedIn dominance for sales
- 78% of social-selling salespeople outperform non-social-selling peers.
- 90% of TOP salespeople already use social selling tools.
- 80%+ of B2B leads from social media come from LinkedIn.
- 700,000+ Sales Navigator users.
- LinkedIn InMail response rates 18-25% vs cold email 3-5%.
- Reps who invest in LinkedIn personal brand see 2-3x higher connection acceptance.

## X/Twitter for sales
- 58% of TECH buyers active on X (vs 31% professional services, 23% manufacturing).
- Some sales influencers use X heavily, but the *prospecting* function lives on LinkedIn.

## Community platforms (newer)
- Pavilion: "private membership for GTM professionals" — Pathfinder AI copilot launched Sept 2025.
- Sales Hacker Community: 166,000 members.
- Clay Community Slack: ~30,000 members; 20K+ are "power users open to roles."
- RepVue: company-level ratings only, NOT individual rep portfolios.

## How sales reps prove track record TODAY
- Self-reported quota %, attainment, rank, team metrics across last 3 years.
- Verified through reference calls with former managers.
- "Win stories" / case studies — but these are company assets, not personal portfolios.
- LinkedIn recommendations and President's Club mentions.

## The gap
- No equivalent of GitHub for salespeople. The closest is Clay community workflows for GTM Engineers, and that's a TINY niche (~5,000 profiles globally).
- Standard sales practice still relies on:
  (a) Verifiable quota attainment (gameable in AI age - whose quota?)
  (b) LinkedIn endorsements (gameable / generic)
  (c) Reference checks (relationships)
- Reps building "AI-orchestrated" stories don't yet have a native venue to display these. They're cobbling together LinkedIn posts and Substacks.

## Implication
Sales has the MOST direct quantifiable performance culture in business — but exactly that culture is being shaken because:
1. "Did you actually drive that revenue or did the AI?" is a new question
2. Pure quota stats are losing signal value
3. The narrative of "how" the rep deploys AI matters more than ever
4. LinkedIn is built around past employment, not orchestration skill

A "show your work" product for sales would need to demonstrate:
- Workflow design (Clay tables, prompt chains)
- Outbound campaigns with results
- AI-tool deployment evidence
- BUT very few reps will be self-motivated to maintain it without strong career incentive.
