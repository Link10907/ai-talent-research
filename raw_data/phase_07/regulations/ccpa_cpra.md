# CCPA / CPRA — Data Collection Notes

## TL;DR (150-250 words)
The California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), significantly amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) effective January 1, 2023, is the primary US state privacy law affecting our product's US users. The most critical issue for our B-side business model: the CCPA's definition of "sale" of personal information is extraordinarily broad. "Sale" includes "selling, renting, releasing, disclosing, disseminating, making available, transferring, or otherwise communicating" personal information to a third party for valuable consideration. If we pass user-derived career data to employers (B-side) in exchange for payment, this almost certainly constitutes a "sale" under CCPA — triggering the requirement to provide a "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" opt-out link and process opt-out requests within 15 business days. The B2B exemption that once sheltered employment data from CCPA expired December 31, 2022 — so employee/professional data is now fully covered. Employment information is NOT a "sensitive personal information" category under CCPA (unlike genetic data, health info, precise geolocation, etc.), but it is regular "personal information" subject to all standard CCPA rights. Users have rights to know, delete, correct, and opt out. The 12-month lookback requirement means data access requests cover the preceding 12 months. Enforcement is by California AG + California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA). Citation range: V634-V636.

## Coverage and Applicability

**Who CCPA/CPRA covers**:
- For-profit businesses that:
  - Have annual gross revenue > $25M, OR
  - Buy/sell personal information of 100,000+ consumers/households annually, OR
  - Derive 50%+ of revenue from selling personal information
- Applies to California residents regardless of where the business is located
- B2B exemption: **expired December 31, 2022** — employment data fully covered since 2023

## Definition of Personal Information (§1798.140(v))

Broadly: "information that identifies, relates to, describes, is reasonably capable of being associated with, or could reasonably be linked, directly or indirectly, with a particular consumer or household."

**Examples directly relevant to our product**:
- Real name, alias, email address → covered
- Professional or employment-related information → covered (Cal. Civ. Code §1798.140(v)(1)(I))
- Education information → covered
- Inferences drawn to create a profile about a person's "professional or employment-related information, preferences, characteristics, psychological trends, predispositions, behavior, attitudes, intelligence, abilities, and aptitudes" → **explicitly covered**

**Critical finding**: Inferences generated by AI to create a profile about a person's professional characteristics are explicitly enumerated as personal information under §1798.140(v)(1)(L).

## Definition of "Sale" (§1798.140(ad))

> "Selling, renting, releasing, disclosing, disseminating, making available, transferring, or otherwise communicating orally, in writing, or by electronic or other means, a consumer's personal information by the business to a third party for monetary or other valuable consideration."

**CPRA addition**: "Share" was added alongside "sell" — covering "sharing" for "cross-context behavioral advertising."

**B-side implication**:
If we pass career profile data (user-derived, aggregated, or AI-enhanced) to an employer in exchange for payment or subscription fee:
- This is a "sale" under CCPA
- We must provide a "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link
- We must honor opt-out requests within 15 business days
- We cannot discriminate against users who opt out

**Possible exception**: If the data transfer is "necessary to perform the services... requested by the consumer" — but this requires careful legal analysis of whether our B-side model can be structured as a consumer-requested service rather than a third-party sale.

## Sensitive Personal Information (§1798.140(ae))

The following categories are "sensitive" under CPRA and subject to stronger protections (right to limit use):

| Category | Sensitive? |
|---|---|
| Social security number | ✅ Yes |
| Financial account credentials | ✅ Yes |
| Precise geolocation | ✅ Yes |
| Health/medical information | ✅ Yes |
| Genetic data | ✅ Yes |
| Biometric data used for identification | ✅ Yes |
| Sexual orientation, sex life | ✅ Yes |
| Racial/ethnic origin, religious/philosophical beliefs, union membership | ✅ Yes |
| **Professional/employment information** | ❌ **No — regular personal info only** |
| **GitHub activity, portfolio data** | ❌ **No — regular personal info only** |
| Email/text message contents | ✅ Yes |

**Finding**: Employment and professional portfolio data is NOT "sensitive personal information" under CCPA — it's regular personal information subject to standard (not heightened) protections. This means our product doesn't trigger the "limit use of sensitive personal information" right specifically, but all other CCPA rights still apply.

## Consumer Rights

| Right | CCPA/CPRA | Our Product Obligation |
|---|---|---|
| Right to Know | §1798.100 | Disclose what personal info we collect, use, sell, and share |
| Right to Delete | §1798.105 | Delete personal info upon request (with exceptions) |
| Right to Correct | §1798.106 (CPRA addition) | Correct inaccurate personal information |
| Right to Opt-Out (of Sale/Sharing) | §1798.120 | Provide opt-out mechanism; honor within 15 business days |
| Right to Limit Use of Sensitive PI | §1798.121 | N/A (professional data not "sensitive") |
| Right to Non-Discrimination | §1798.125 | Cannot penalize users who exercise rights |
| Right to Know (12-month lookback) | §1798.130 | Data access requests cover preceding 12 months |

## 12-Month Retention Rule

§1798.130 requires that requests to know (what data was collected) cover the **preceding 12-month period**. Businesses must respond within 45 days (extensible by additional 45 days with notice).

**Product implication**: Must maintain audit-trail of what data was collected per user, when, and for what purpose, covering at least 12 months of history. Cannot delete historical processing records on-demand before 12 months.

## B2B Exemption (Expired)

The CCPA originally included temporary exemptions for:
- Employee data collected by employers (§1798.145(m)) — **expired January 1, 2023**
- Business-to-business communications data (§1798.145(n)) — **expired January 1, 2023**

Since 2023: California residents acting in their capacity as employees, contractors, or business contacts have full CCPA rights. Our product's processing of professional career data is fully covered.

## Enforcement

**California Attorney General (CAG)**: Enforces CCPA; can fine up to $2,500/unintentional violation or $7,500/intentional violation
**California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA)**: Created by CPRA; began enforcement in 2023; can issue regulations and enforcement actions

**Notable enforcement**: The CPPA has signaled active enforcement interest in AI-driven profiling and automated decision-making.
